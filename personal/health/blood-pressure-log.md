> **Important:** This is a personal tracking tool, not medical advice. Reference ranges below are general guidance; your own targets may differ. Discuss your readings and any concerns with your doctor or pharmacist.

## How to Take an Accurate Reading

For consistent, comparable numbers:

1. **Rest first** — sit quietly for 5 minutes before measuring. No caffeine, exercise, or smoking for 30 minutes beforehand.
2. **Sit correctly** — back supported, feet flat on the floor, legs uncrossed.
3. **Position the arm** — bare upper arm resting on a table, cuff at heart height.
4. **Stay still and quiet** — do not talk during the measurement.
5. **Take two readings**, one minute apart, and record both (or their average).
6. **Same time each day** — ideally morning and evening, before medication and food.

## Reference Ranges (general adult guidance)

| Category | Systolic (top) | | Diastolic (bottom) |
|----------|----------------|---|--------------------|
| **Normal** | below 120 | and | below 80 |
| **Elevated** | 120–129 | and | below 80 |
| **High — Stage 1** | 130–139 | or | 80–89 |
| **High — Stage 2** | 140 or higher | or | 90 or higher |
| **Crisis** | higher than 180 | and/or | higher than 120 |

> **Warning:** A reading over **180 / 120** with symptoms such as chest pain, shortness of breath, weakness, or vision change is a medical emergency — seek urgent care immediately. Thresholds vary between national guidelines (e.g. ESC/ESH vs AHA); follow the targets your doctor sets for you.
