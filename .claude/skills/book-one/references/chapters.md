## Synopses

**Ch 1 — The Boy Who Signed (1603).** Three-day storm. Tom (22) collects his
Company contract from Harcourt's counting room, where an unnamed copyist —
Matthew Bell — asks whether he would sign a debt designed never to close.
Silas Rook kicks in the Pelican's door dragging Mara, blood-soaked, accusing
her of murdering "a Company clerk" — Bell. Tom blocks the door, takes a
backhand, stays. The room reasons its way to the truth: Arthur's wind
arithmetic (no scream audible over a North Sea gale), the practised underhand
grip Pell exposes, Barnaby's dying observation and the pale breaking-shed pine
dust Carter finds in Rook's boot welt. Josiah's lynch talk is crushed by Pell,
Carter and Elias (the Admiralty basket threat). Ned states water-law: "she is
owed a landing." Rook lunges, is faced down by the room, escapes into the
storm — twelve people wait one fatal second each for another to begin. Tom and
Ned carry Bell in from the shed; he dies on the centre table after "Anne" and
one "no". Mara gives Bell's confession page to Maggie: names, manufactured
debts, herself entered between cordage and spoiled timber ("Mara de Sousa —
Portuguese servant — transfer pending"), Tom's father's £11 6s 4d marked
"Account disputed. No discharge permitted." Arthur names the dead man in full.
The page goes into the fault beneath the bar. "You're going to die for a
clove, Tom."

**Ch 2 — Dutch Courage (1603).** The Dragon's lads arrive from the first
fleet, editing their story (pepper and gold, not the 100+ dead). Pell's toll:
480 sailed, over a hundred dead before India. Hendricks the Dutch waterman
warns Tom about the VOC ("wolves"), tells of his brother Pieter who died
raving about the islands; delivers the book's thesis: a room can beat a bully,
"No room has ever stood up to a paper." Maggie explains how the debt walks
from Levant seal to East India seal. Tom leaves his mother's brass thimble:
"Till I'm back." Genever for courage. Maggie cuts Tom's notch before dawn —
the only notch she ever cuts early. Anne Bell comes at morning, refuses
Arthur's copy and burns it ("One copy in a widow's house... is evidence
against a widow"); tells Tom "remember there was a man at the other end of the
ink." Tom and Arthur go to Harcourt: Harcourt's bloodless menace ("Bell's
confusion has cost me twice"), Arthur is offered — and takes — Bell's chair,
witnesses Tom's contract; Tom signs. Five days later the coroner's finding:
wilful murder by Silas Rook, whereabouts unknown; Harcourt absent; Mara
reduced to "foreign female, name uncertain"; Maggie forces one added sentence
about the common table. "The ships go out. The boys sign."

**Ch 3 — The Soot and the Roof (1603).** Mara's own night: she never uses the
bed, folds the blanket precisely (bookkeeping, not gratitude), leaves Bell's
page below by choice, exits over the lean-to roof barefoot (one print on sill,
one in soot). Listens for the tide, evades the watch, freezes at a crane
shaped like Rook. At the river stairs Hendricks — in dock-Portuguese — offers
to finish the dead clerk's plan: the man at the kerk (Dutch church), a timber
boat. "Because the keeper stood in front of you." He does not watch her
decide. Aboard the fluyt *De Hoop*, deacon Joos de Vries won't enter a lone
woman passenger; Mara: "Write me as cargo. This time it is I who say so."
Hendricks gives her Pieter's sea-wool coat and journal packet ("Learn the
words... Then burn it, or keep it"), one pull of genever, and the warning:
"Know them for wolves and you will do good business. Know them only for wolves
and you will become one. Hold both." First Dutch word: *hout*, wood.

**Ch 4 — The Steward's Search (1603).** Anne's four hours before Harcourt's
steward arrives: the body home, the dangerous scraps burned (including the
strip in daughter Joan's primer), the "armour" papers (rent receipt, baker's
tally, pawn ticket) left visible. She makes the steward search aloud, itemised,
with a signed receipt — he leaves with one empty satchel. "Legal eats less
bread than theft." Maggie arrives with broth; the two women begin their
twenty-year account. Anne's rule for the Pelican: "Use the receipt first."
The girls learn: Name. Master. Thing. Authority. Receipt.

**Ch 5 — The Language of Paper (1603–1604).** Amsterdam: customs turns *goods*
into *dienstmaagd* (maidservant) — two strokes carry her from cargo into
service, and Mara files the shape. Joos bonds her passage as servant to the
surgeon's widow Lysbeth van Aalst and son Willem (7); Mara's three conditions
(full name entered, no advance, Pieter's packet her own property). The voyage
teaches by subtraction; she learns Dutch numbers-before-mercy, wins the
water-ration dispute by counting the rationer's pours (five entered as six),
Lysbeth shields her ("You will not turn my son's shame into her debt").
Willem touches the packet; "Mine" — Lysbeth backs her ownership; grammar
lesson: *van mij, eigen, toestemming*. Pieter's book read at last: the sailor's
parts, then the margins — a hostage, a burned storehouse, two men struck from
the ration list before dying — and the fever's refrain "*Make another.*" At
Bantam Mara settles the twelve-sacks pepper dispute, wins meals + bolt + her
name beneath every translation, and makes her first defiant duplicate for the
broker Iskandar: "This one belongs to the man whose pepper it records."

**Ch 6 — The Counter-Ledger (1603–1611).** Maggie's system from inside: the
fault beneath the bar (keepsakes = wagers, notches = verdicts; "The keepsake
records the leaving. A notch records the failure to return"). Tom's is the
only notch cut early. The horn button boy comes home and learns why no cut was
made. Barnaby dies by Whitsun 1604; his stool stays empty by unspoken treaty.
Pell ships on the *Ascension* 1608, leaves no keepsake; when the Company lists
the ship unreturned, Maggie cuts his notch. Elias dies the following winter;
"what Elias would have made of it" becomes the river's measure. Arthur's last
chalked wager fades and is washed off. The bar is lengthened; Maggie has the
joiner preserve the old length and its fault. "The ships keep the wage. I keep
the leaving."

**Ch 7 — The Teak Desk (1605–1612).** On day 31 Mara walks out of the Dutch
loge — the factor's "protection" was the price of proving useful — and sets
her own rate from a rented room above a scale-maker (bolt on her side).
Translates a captured Portuguese victualling dispatch knowing it dooms the
ship; the prisoner calls her traitor; she answers Lysbeth: "Because I wanted
what the money would buy" — the teak desk, too heavy to run with, too heavy
to take. Lysbeth and Willem sail west free of claims; two oilskin packets
(Pieter's fever pages, letter for Hendricks, her ended-bond account) travel by
two ships — "I do not trust water." 1608 court coup and 1609 debtor release
teach the axiom: "Paper records power. It does not replace it." By 1612 she is
the woman at the teak desk whose two versions agree, in dark silk, in her own
ground-floor counting room between the two Companies. "I write the rate."

**Ch 8 — The Return (1612).** Nine years on: new flagstones, longer bar,
Swally won. Tom returns at 31, hardened boatswain, pike scar, father's spare
whistle (father dead 1608, physician's fee on the account). Sits unasked on
Barnaby's stool — "the room lets him, and both of those things mean
something." Blue cotton with red climbing flower: "Table cloth." / "For which
table?" Meat pie, mutual inventory of scars, the hinge repaired together. Then
Jack Mercer, 16, cooper's son, father crushed under Riga pine at Carter's
yard, £4 debt. Maggie's demolition: "There is always a *with or without me*";
"I think you should stop calling the taking a rescue"; "Books are made by
hands." Carter: "The debt is lawful." / "So was the woman in Bell's book."
Maggie drops the thimble at Tom's feet — "You're Master Harcourt. You're
Silas Rook... Don't come back until you remember what a human life is worth."
Tom doesn't pick it up; Maggie retrieves it to the fault. She feeds Jack the
pie and arms him with questions ("So the clerk has to hear himself answer").
The hiring room: Jack asks twice, the queue starts asking, Arthur caps the kit
at 12s, values replacements at condition-when-lost; Tom puts 2s/month of his
own wage against the debt ("instruction allowance"), credited to Carter until
the £4 closes, then to Jack. Fourteen years of absence begin.

**Ch 9 — The Boy in the Rigging (1613).** Jack's voyage out. First climbs
("One hand. One foot. Then the others"), the pale buntline and the open-hand
blow that breaks fear's grip, the ration-line fight (victual book vs wage
book: "Unless the Company has found a way for half a boy to do a whole watch,
weigh eleven"), the beetle joke that buys him a place, the flux that kills
nineteen (Ellis, Brown, Cooper, Venn — Tom forces full names into the book,
stops the surgeon bleeding the dehydrated), and the Madagascar squall: Jack
reads the backed foretopsail, ten seconds, the mast goes, Jack cuts Tom free
of the parted backstay with Tom's own knife ("Not across. With the lay!"),
they pull Pritchard (broken jaw) from under the sail. Jury rig; the inquiry
records both truths because Tom makes it. First praise: "Good."

**Ch 10 — The Pay Table (1613–1614).** Bantam green water; Tom's market
languages; the woman rigger teaches Jack the buried splice by touch ("find
the join"). The capstan crushes two of Jack's fingers rushing pepper before
rain; the surgeon's dressing is charged 6d against his wage. Homeward, Tom
reconstructs the debt: Carter's £4 closed two months before the Cape; eleven
months of half-wages plus two instruction payments vanished between correct
books. At the pay table the paymaster stonewalls until Tom takes him by the
collar — "the only language in the room the paymaster has not prepared a form
to delay." The money is "found in seven minutes" (temporary balance,
unallocated receipt, carried remainder, discrepancy awaiting advice). The
clerk tries to charge the sixpence twice. Jack returns Tom's two post-closure
payments; keeps the rest; refuses a second voyage — "Where I can see both
ends of the rope" — and signs with the Deptford quartermaster, making him put
the tool-ownership term on the page. Tom cannot walk the short road to the
Pelican: "I would be going to show her this."

**Ch 11 — Arthur in the Chair (1603–1614).** Arthur's whole arc. Bell's chair
too low; the inventory; the weather book turned upside down and started from
the back — the private book ("Matthew Bell. Dead before the account closed").
He becomes the Company's best euphemist (a carried balance becomes "an amount
awaiting advice") while numbering private entries no index holds. 1608: enters
the additions to the dead Fletcher's account (physician who did not save him,
burial his son did not attend) — crosses out the accusation, writes the exact
version, which is worse. 1612: Jack's questions reach across nine years; the
small mercies at the rail. 1614: the pay-table irregularity report; asked to
fix the form, he drafts the discharge clause that would legalise Jack's eleven
lost months for the next boy — "The true book is hidden... The other will
make the harm orderly before he arrives." The ink stain becomes "the part of
the work that stays."

**Ch 12 — The Lone Machine (1614–1620).** Tom signs the Surat berth minutes
after closing his dead mother's rooms (her letter travelled seven months and
no ocean; no thimble among the effects — it is under Maggie's bar). Surat
mastery: every correction strengthens the machine (safer landing → thirty more
bales; honest purser → men re-sign). The missing bale: a copying error nearly
hangs the porter Yusuf; Tom forces the false statement struck and signed, then
builds the double tally — the system keeps the lesson longer than the man.
Feeds hungry hired boys before assessment ("It makes the answer cost less").
His father dies three deaths (the bed, the spare whistle, the account that
keeps walking). The 1620 cloth order filled perfectly: "Nothing in the
operation is false. Nothing in it belongs to him except the success... It fits
him exactly. For now, he fits it back."

**Ch 13 — The Echo (1620–1622).** Tom's body keeps 1603: never sits with his
back to a door, touches the scar under strain, frees a rice-thief child and
steps out of the doorway. Refuses to sign the blank "declaration of ordinary
loss" before quantities are attached; the machine answers with a clause
permitting estimates. One day of shore leave: the cobbler, the awning knot,
lentils shared at a fair rate — he flees rest into repair. Seven letters to
Maggie written, none sent (the fifth, "You were right", burned for the demand
hidden in it; the seventh — "I remembered before I knew what remembering
required" — folded unsigned). Teaches a frozen boy with voice instead of hand:
"Which line? / Where does it lead? / Then go aft." "The sea repeats nothing
exactly. It carries the sound forward changed."

**Ch 14 — News from the Sea (1614–1623).** Maggie's method: food first, then
the same questions in order (Which ship? Who did not come off it? Names.),
then the habits no manifest keeps. Three paid-off hands and the carrot-sorter.
Tom arrives in pieces (hardest boatswain east of the Cape / fed twelve starved
boys / "Company to the marrow"); Maggie makes every witness give the other
part — "keep both parts." She recovers Yusuf's near-name from a guard who only
remembered "porter." She refuses to send any letter ("A truth sent for the
purpose of producing an answer is already becoming a bargain") and lodges no
hearsay in the fault: "Hearsay is not a true copy, however badly the heart
wants to keep it." Jack's standing answer if Tom comes west: "Food first."

**Ch 15 — The Intersecting Web (1614–1623).** Mara builds the network: second
teak desk to Aminah at Amboyna (who answers by raising the rate), the Makassar
bookkeeper, the Masulipatam oilskin box, the Patani widow who memorises.
Rules: she buys work, never people; pays even when news loses to the monsoon;
names stay unsold ("the report must arrive again next month"). String across
the counting room: "It marks agreements men believe are separate." Banda
1621: six hands go silent; she writes the missing names with last-known-alive
dates — "Because power should have to step over them." Courier Rafi drowns;
payment owed, entered under his full name. The forged seal answered in person;
the new seal with the deliberate broken line. Daniel Vale enters as a margin
note; she circles the name ("He names the porters"), refuses to let the
Company own his copies, sets the rate, answers his question — "Both. Ownership
is the dispute. Do not edit it out." Early 1623 the pages assemble into "a
door being bolted"; her warning to Aminah arrives too late; the harbour list
shows one factor "retained... to Batavia." She moves the string.

**Ch 16 — The Factor (1621–1623).** Daniel Vale, 26, Rochester clerk, goes
east "to come back worth a story"; Nell's parting orders ("Write everything
down"). Amboyna: the green steepness, the clove sweetness, the castle's
arithmetic (twelve English, four hundred Dutch, Japanese soldiers drilling).
Towerson, the best man he ever serves: "The treaty holds, gentlemen. We are
merchants." The rice margin note that defines him ("not brave... unable,
physically, the way some men cannot carry a tune, to initial a number he
watched be false"). The broker's question and rate; Towerson's distinction:
"Paper a man buys with his own coin is the only place his eyes are the ones
that were there." The audit of small things — searches doubled, seals warmed,
lamp oil, the interpreter, night-boat ban, the copied stores list: "six
reasonable explanations standing in a row." Towerson refuses to spend twelve
men's safety on the arithmetic of lamp oil. The garrison account and the
double-entried Japanese soldiers (wages in one book, suspicion in the other).
Last packet: "Both retained." Then the young soldier's question about the
watch, two nights of sounds from the castle, the courteous invitation, and the
arrests — Daniel counts 111 steps to the gate, "the last count he will finish
for a long time."

**Ch 17 — The Coral Room (1623).** The torture chapter. No window ("a window
is a clock"). Pre-written confession; the pen writes only the waited-for
answers — "Truth is being transcribed backward. He is a copying man. They are
making him into a page." Waterboarding rendered exactly. Through the coral
wall, the fact of Towerson holds two days; the quiet is worse; Daniel loses
the count — the one thing he never loses — permanently in part. He signs the
harvested confession ("The water took hours. The signature took the thing the
hours were for"). He never says Nell's name — "a man can keep what nobody
knows to ask for." Two men look at him like cargo; one word — "Batavia" — and
no reason, ever. "It was the only mercy anyone showed at Amboyna, and it was
not mercy. It was paperwork."

**Ch 18 — Amboyna (1623).** The news reaches Surat as three accounts: Dutch
(order as proof), English ("They used water until the men would own any design
put to them"), commercial (losses, and the pivot recommendation). Tom lays the
commercial page beneath the others — "Paper order is not justice. It is the
only correction available in the room." The Company copies the event into
versions per appetite; Tom has the crew copy carry the ten English names
before the assurance, plus "and ten Japanese men whose names are not given in
the account," and keeps a private list with ten blank lines ("names not yet
recovered... That is what it is for"). Grief becomes cotton within a month;
"Amboyna may be the making of Surat" nearly earns a man a broken jaw. The
unsealed Bantam packet: rice for more prisoners than the sentence admits, and
the note — one English factor retained, sent to Batavia. Tom adds the line
"English factor. Name unknown. Batavia" and "begins travelling toward the
word."

**Ch 19 — The Widow's Years (1603–1623).** Anne across twenty years: widowhood
as a trade with tools. The broth-and-mending economy with Maggie that neither
will price ("An honest blank is still an entry" is Maggie's side; Anne leaves
a blank beside Maggie's name in the household book). The 1612 scene: after
Maggie throws Tom out, Anne audits the sentence — "He is not Matthew... Right
is not the same as exact"; "do not turn my dead husband into a cudgel because
you are frightened of losing a living man." By 1623 women bring Anne their
Company claims; the Ratcliff widow's wage claim executed like a campaign (the
pay-office waiting room, the women's shared procedure, "Is examination the
shelf behind you?"). Her creed: "Some accounts are kept because payment is
coming. Some are kept so nobody can say nothing was owed."

**Ch 20 — Batavia (1623–1626).** Mara moves the desk to Batavia and works
Daniel's release as arithmetic, not mercy: "A prisoner without a charge is not
a prisoner. He is an unclosed entry... Close it while closing is cheap." The
fiscal requires "an English signature and an English deck." Tom has spent
fourteen months volunteering toward the harbour line; the English council
prices Daniel below the dead ("Ten dead men make a massacre. Nine dead men
and a witness under water make a proceeding"). Tom and Mara finally meet —
"One long look. Not love. Recognition." Neither spends the other's name. He
corrects his list to *Daniel Vale* at her desk. No rate: "Some work I pay for
myself... There is a room in London that reads accounts truly... take him
through that door and give him to the keeper." Genever passed on, Hendricks's
words with it. The release: Daniel watches Tom *read before signing* — the
first man since the coral room. The passage of small mercies: the turned
chair, the covered cup always on the left, the silenced lantern, food pushed
across with one foot, "Can you walk that far? / Then that is all we are doing
today." Surat desk for a year; the form's word "examined"; "I cannot"; Tom
fixes the shipping lists. The eighth letter to Maggie "is not paper. It walks
the deck at his side."

**Ch 21 — The Man Who Came Back Wrong (1626).** Tom (45) walks Daniel into
the Pelican after fourteen years; the silence of 1612 stands in the room. Ale
for Daniel unasked; on the second evening a pie for Tom, no ale — "The pie is
not forgiveness. It is older than forgiveness, and stubborner." The Jack
accounting between Maggie and Tom. Daniel tells Amboyna on the fourth day;
Carter pours the house's genever onto the flagstones — and the old Dutch
skipper (unnamed; almost certainly Hendricks) leaves his cup half finished
and goes. Carter's grief slides toward use ("from *what did they do to you*
toward *what can I do with it*") and Daniel stops answering. The English
deposition: "The prisoner states that he was examined with some severity" —
"he has now been drowned twice." Pryce's offer (Surat at double pay, family
debts cleared — the list in Arthur's unmistakable hand — for "quiet"); Daniel:
"Pay the dead men's families... I've already paid what this cost once, and
I'm not selling it a second time." The true copy written at the bar, full
name, placed *beside* Bell's page — Maggie: "Do not make the smaller thing
large because it is the thing we managed." Tom sees the thimble; leaves it.
Nell arrives ("She knew him by his hands"): "You write the letter... You
don't make that call for them." Jack's adult account: both truths — "About
another boatswain being worse? Yes. About that making the debt a kindness?
No. Both can be true." Maggie sets the thimble on the bar — "Till you're
back" — returns it to the gap, and pours Tom a pint: "given by the keeper to
a man who did wrong, heard why, changed what he could and did not come home
to perform the changing for applause."

**Ch 22 — The South Land (1629–1630).** 1630 frame: Mara's packet reaches the
Pelican addressed in four hands "For the keeper of the Pelican, Wapping
Wall." Flashback: 1628 Batavia, Mara finds Rook on the *Batavia* muster,
files the exact warning ("He is not to be placed over unarmed persons"); the
white-cuffed factor annotates it "experienced disciplinarian... useful" — "You
are making the danger into a qualification." She confronts Rook at the
gangplank. The wreck (June 1629), Cornelisz's mutiny, Hayes's coral fort,
Pelsaert's return on the *Sardam*; Mara translates exactly — corrects herself
mid-word from a verb of killing to "Carried," accepting the mother's hatred:
"A true record that serves vengeance by changing one word is the same
instrument with a different owner." She makes Rook hear Matthew Bell's name;
argues against the island gallows — "A rope is an English mercy, Commander" —
not as mercy: the warning must be read beside the dead before the Council.
Rook's half-truth ("She wrote what I was and your gentlemen hired me with
it... *Useful.* That is their word, not mine") is translated intact. The
Council condemns him; the factor keeps his post — "Silas alone is made to
contain the whole meaning of Silas, because a body can be punished and a
method cannot." 1630, Wapping: Anne and her grown daughter read the letter;
Anne on Silas knowing her name: "A name may be love in one mouth and leverage
in another." The third paper joins the fault. Maggie's twin verdicts: "Do not
steal Silas Rook's guilt to make our failure feel larger and therefore
nobler," and "What Mara gave Silas was a sentence... Do not confuse it with
restoring the dead."

**Epilogue — Last Orders (1635).** Tom, in his fifties, debt long closed, pays
off a last ship and walks west. Jack's name is on the Deptford gate ("the man
contracted to keep the lifting tackle honest"). At the Pelican a new keeper —
Joan — serves the pie before the news, per Maggie's standing orders ("If a
scarred man named Fletcher ever comes through that door — feed him first.
Then tell him"). Maggie died at the turn of 1633, seventy, in the chair by
the hearth, fire laid, counter wiped, nothing owing. Thyme in Joan's crust:
"An imitation would have been unbearable. This is inheritance instead."
Churchyard: "keeper of this parish." The boy with the *Discovery* contract:
Tom reads the paper aloud, gives the questions, and "leaves the decision
where it belongs, which is not rescue and not enough and the most he has
learned to do." Joan shows him the fault: notches, three folded accounts,
the thimble. "Till I'm back, the wager ran. He is back, and she is not, and
the thimble stays — because taking it would close the account, and some
accounts are better kept open." The blue-cloth dialogue replayed with Joan,
smiling. "Goodnight, Maggie." — "Last orders are called in every house on
the river... The ships go out. The boys sign. The Pelican pours."

**Reference — The Wapping Twelve (book-one-character-bible).** In-world
ledger of the twelve souls of the 1603 night, "the room's own" numbering,
plus The Defendant (Mara), The Dead Man (Bell), and the Rupture-and-Mending
spine. Framed to be read "the way you would read the last page of a mystery:
afterwards." All surnames but Rook's are declared the book's own invention.
