# Book One — The Venture (1603–1635): Chapter Ledger

The live reader's Book One as deployed at `nj22az.github.io/the-front-row-seat/`
(13 narrative units + part page + character bible, ~100,000 words). Reader order
is authoritative and comes from `readerBookIds` in
`the-front-row-seat/omnibus-config.js`. Page ids are historical and do NOT sort
into reading order — always follow the sequence below.

**Consolidated from the original 23-unit structure** (see
`projects/literary/EIC/editorial/chapter-consolidation-plan.md` for the full
rationale) — nine of the original chapters merged into four, each merge
following a direct narrative continuity (same POV character across a time
jump, two threads converging on one event, or a survey chapter that was
entirely build-up to an adjacent scene). No content was cut; total word count
is unchanged. Four chapters — The Widow's Years, The Man Who Came Back Wrong,
The South Land, and the epilogue — were left standalone.

## Synopses

**Ch 1 — The Boy Who Signed (1603).** Three-day storm. Tom (22) collects his
Company contract from Harcourt's counting room, where an unnamed copyist —
Matthew Bell — asks whether he would sign a debt designed never to close.
Silas Rook kicks in the Pelican's door dragging Maria, blood-soaked, accusing
her of murdering "a Company clerk" — Bell. Tom blocks the door, takes a
backhand, stays. The room reasons its way to the truth: Arthur's wind
arithmetic (no scream audible over a North Sea gale), the practised underhand
grip Pell exposes, Barnaby's dying observation and the pale breaking-shed pine
dust Carter finds in Rook's boot welt. Josiah's lynch talk is crushed by Pell,
Carter and Elias (the Admiralty basket threat). Ned states water-law: "she is
owed a landing." Rook lunges, is faced down by the room, escapes into the
storm. Tom and Ned carry Bell in from the shed; he dies on the centre table
after "Anne" and one "no". Maria gives Bell's confession page to Maggie.
Arthur names the dead man in full. The page goes into the fault beneath the
bar. *Continues same night:* the Dragon's lads arrive, editing their own
story; Hendricks the Dutch waterman delivers the book's thesis ("No room has
ever stood up to a paper") and warns Tom about the VOC. Tom leaves his
mother's brass thimble: "Till I'm back." Maggie cuts Tom's notch before dawn
— the only notch she ever cuts early. Anne Bell comes at morning, refuses
Arthur's copy and burns it; tells Tom to remember there was a man at the
other end of the ink. Arthur is offered — and takes — Bell's chair. Five days
later the coroner's finding: wilful murder by Silas Rook, whereabouts
unknown; Harcourt absent; Maria reduced to "foreign female, name uncertain."
"You're going to die for a clove, Tom." / "The ships go out. The boys sign."

**Ch 2 — What the Women Did (1603).** Maria's own night: she never uses the
bed, exits over the lean-to roof barefoot, evades the watch, freezes at a
crane shaped like Rook. Hendricks offers to finish the dead clerk's plan; a
timber boat, Pieter's sea-wool coat and journal packet, one pull of genever,
and the warning: "Know them for wolves and you will do good business. Know
them only for wolves and you will become one. Hold both." First Dutch word:
*hout*, wood. *Same night, Shadwell:* Anne's four hours before Harcourt's
steward arrives — the body home, the dangerous scraps burned, the "armour"
papers left visible. She makes the steward search aloud, itemised, with a
signed receipt. Maggie arrives with broth; the two women begin their
twenty-year account. Anne's rule for the Pelican: "Use the receipt first."

**Ch 3 — Maria's Passage East (1603–1612).** Amsterdam: customs turns *goods*
into *dienstmaagd* (maidservant). Joos bonds her passage as servant to
Lysbeth van Aalst and son Willem; her three conditions (full name entered, no
advance, Pieter's packet her own property). She wins the water-ration
dispute by counting the rationer's pours. At Bantam Maria settles the
twelve-sacks pepper dispute, wins meals + bolt + her name beneath every
translation, and makes her first defiant duplicate for the broker Iskandar.
*Continues, 1605–1612:* on day 31 Maria walks out of the Dutch loge — the
factor's "protection" was the price of proving useful — and sets her own
rate from a rented room. Translates a captured Portuguese dispatch knowing
it dooms the ship; answers the accusation of treachery: "Because I wanted
what the money would buy" — the teak desk. Lysbeth and Willem sail west free
of claims. By 1612 she is the woman at the teak desk whose two versions
agree, in dark silk, between the two Companies. "I write the rate."

**Ch 4 — The Return (1603–1612).** Maggie's counter-ledger system from
inside: the fault beneath the bar, keepsakes as wagers, notches as verdicts.
Tom's is the only notch cut early. Barnaby dies by Whitsun 1604; his stool
stays empty by unspoken treaty. Pell ships on the *Ascension* 1608 and is
lost; Elias dies the following winter; Arthur's last chalked wager fades and
is washed off. The bar is lengthened; Maggie has the joiner preserve the old
length and its fault. *Continues, 1612:* nine years on, Tom returns at 31,
hardened boatswain, pike scar. Blue cotton with red climbing flower: "For
which table?" Then Jack Mercer, 16, cooper's son, father crushed under Riga
pine, £4 debt. Maggie's demolition of Tom's rescue-language: "There is
always a *with or without me*"; drops the thimble at his feet. Tom doesn't
pick it up; Maggie retrieves it to the fault. The hiring room: Arthur caps
Jack's kit, Tom puts 2s/month of his own wage against the debt. Fourteen
years of absence begin.

**Ch 5 — The Pay Table (1603–1614).** Jack's first voyage: first climbs, the
ration-line fight, the flux that kills nineteen, the Madagascar squall —
Jack reads the backed foretopsail, cuts Tom free of the parted backstay with
Tom's own knife. First praise: "Good." *Continues, 1613–1614:* Bantam green
water, the capstan crushes two of Jack's fingers, the surgeon's dressing
charged against his wage. At the pay table Tom takes the paymaster by the
collar; eleven months of vanished half-wages are "found in seven minutes."
Jack refuses a second voyage — "Where I can see both ends of the rope" —
and signs with the Deptford quartermaster on his own terms. *A second,
older account runs underneath the whole thing, begun the same week Bell
died:* Arthur's arc — Bell's chair too low, the private book started the
night Bell died, becoming the Company's best euphemist while numbering
entries no index holds. 1608: enters the dead Fletcher's additions. 1612:
Jack's questions reach across nine years. 1614: drafts the discharge clause
that legalises Jack's eleven lost months for the next boy — "The true book
is hidden... The other will make the harm orderly before he arrives."

**Ch 6 — Tom at Surat (1614–1622).** Tom signs the Surat berth minutes after
closing his dead mother's rooms. Surat mastery: every correction
strengthens the machine. The missing bale nearly hangs the porter Yusuf;
Tom builds the double tally. His father dies three deaths (the bed, the
spare whistle, the account that keeps walking). *Continues, 1620–1622:*
Tom's body keeps 1603 — never sits with his back to a door, touches the
scar under strain. One day of shore leave: he flees rest into repair. Seven
letters to Maggie written, none sent. Teaches a frozen boy with voice
instead of hand. "The sea repeats nothing exactly. It carries the sound
forward changed."

**Ch 7 — The Years Between (1614–1623).** Maggie's method with paid-off
sailors: food first, then the same questions in order. Tom arrives in
pieces — hardest boatswain east of the Cape, fed twelve starved boys,
"Company to the marrow." Maggie refuses to send any letter east: "A truth
sent for the purpose of producing an answer is already becoming a
bargain." *The same nine years run a second account, on the other side of
the ocean, in a hand Maggie has never seen:* Maria builds her spy network —
second teak desk to Aminah at Amboyna, the Makassar bookkeeper, the Patani
widow. Banda 1621: six hands go silent; she writes the missing names with
last-known-alive dates. Daniel Vale enters as a margin note; she circles
the name. Early 1623 the pages assemble into "a door being bolted"; the
harbour list shows one factor "retained... to Batavia."

**Ch 8 — Amboyna (1621–1623).** Daniel Vale, 26, goes east "to come back
worth a story." Amboyna: Towerson, the best man he ever serves — "The treaty
holds, gentlemen. We are merchants." The rice margin note that defines him:
"not brave... unable, physically, the way some men cannot carry a tune, to
initial a number he watched be false." The audit of small things — searches
doubled, seals warmed, the interpreter, night-boat ban: "six reasonable
explanations standing in a row." Last packet: "Both retained." Then the
arrests — Daniel counts 111 steps to the gate. *Continues directly into the
torture chapter:* no window ("a window is a clock"); the pre-written
confession; waterboarding rendered exactly. He signs the harvested
confession. He never says Nell's name. One word — "Batavia" — and no reason,
ever. "It was the only mercy anyone showed at Amboyna, and it was not mercy.
It was paperwork."

**Ch 9 — The Widow's Years (1603–1623).** *Unchanged from the original.*
Anne across twenty years: widowhood as a trade with tools. The
broth-and-mending economy with Maggie that neither will price. The 1612
scene: after Maggie throws Tom out, Anne audits the sentence — "Right is
not the same as exact." By 1623 women bring Anne their Company claims; the
Ratcliff widow's wage claim executed like a campaign. Her creed: "Some
accounts are kept because payment is coming. Some are kept so nobody can
say nothing was owed."

**Ch 10 — Batavia (1623–1626).** The news of Amboyna reaches Surat as three
accounts; Tom lays the commercial page beneath the others. The Company
copies the event into versions per appetite; Tom keeps a private list with
ten blank lines — "names not yet recovered." Now Tom begins travelling
toward the word. *Continues, 1623–1626:* Maria moves the desk to Batavia
and works Daniel's release as arithmetic, not mercy: "A prisoner without a
charge is not a prisoner. He is an unclosed entry." Tom and Maria finally
meet — "One long look. Not love. Recognition." The release: Daniel watches
Tom read before signing — the first man since the coral room. The eighth
letter to Maggie "is not paper. It walks the deck at his side."

**Ch 11 — The Man Who Came Back Wrong (1626).** *Unchanged.* Tom walks
Daniel into the Pelican after fourteen years. Daniel tells Amboyna on the
fourth day; Carter pours the house's genever onto the flagstones. Pryce's
offer for "quiet" — Daniel refuses. The true copy written at the bar,
placed beside Bell's page. Nell arrives. Jack's adult account: both truths
can be true. Maggie sets the thimble on the bar — "Till you're back" —
returns it to the gap.

**Ch 12 — The South Land (1629–1630).** *Unchanged.* Maria finds Rook on the
*Batavia* muster, files the exact warning; the factor annotates it
"useful." The wreck, Cornelisz's mutiny, Pelsaert's return. Maria translates
exactly, corrects herself mid-word from a verb of killing to "Carried."
Argues against the island gallows — not as mercy but so the warning is read
beside the dead. Asks Pelsaert to put Rook ashore alone, without supplies —
a fate the record deliberately never resolves. Anne on Silas knowing her
name: "A name may be love in one mouth and leverage in another."

**Epilogue — Last Orders (1635).** *Unchanged.* Tom, in his fifties, pays
off a last ship and walks west. At the Pelican a new keeper — Joan —
serves the pie before the news, per Maggie's standing orders. Maggie died
at the turn of 1633. The boy with the *Discovery* contract: Tom reads the
paper aloud and leaves the decision where it belongs. Joan shows him the
fault: notches, three folded accounts, the thimble. "Till I'm back, the
wager ran. He is back, and she is not, and the thimble stays."

**Reference — The Wapping Twelve (book-one-character-bible).** In-world
ledger of the twelve souls of the 1603 night, "the room's own" numbering,
plus The Defendant (Maria), The Dead Man (Bell), and the Rupture-and-Mending
spine. Framed to be read "the way you would read the last page of a mystery:
afterwards." All surnames but Rook's are declared the book's own invention.
