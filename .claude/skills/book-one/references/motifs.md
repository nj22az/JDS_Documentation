# Book One — Objects, Motifs, Voice

The furniture of the book is load-bearing. Objects carry the accounts the
characters cannot speak; get one wrong and the whole ledger rings false.

## The counter-ledger (the fault beneath the bar)

A narrow fault in the old oak where spilled ale never reaches. Contents by
1635, in order of arrival:

1. **The notches** — one per boy the sea did not return. A keepsake records
   the leaving; a notch records the failure to return. Tom's is the only
   notch ever cut early (the night he signed); Maggie never cuts early again.
2. **Matthew Bell's account** (1603) — blood-marked original, his full name
   on the outside in Arthur's hand. The confession of a man who helped build
   the machine and died trying to take one woman and several debts out of it.
3. **Tom's brass thimble** (1603) — his mother's. Left "Till I'm back";
   thrown at his feet in 1612; retrieved by Maggie the same night; shown in
   1626 ("Till you're back"); touched and left in 1635. Never taken back:
   "taking it would close the account, and some accounts are better kept
   open."
4. **Daniel Vale's true copy of Amboyna** (1626) — placed *beside* Bell's
   page, not behind it. "One wrote before the machine killed him. One wrote
   after it failed."
5. **Maria's packet** (1630) — her retained warning against Rook, the
   certified copy of the factor's margin ("experienced disciplinarian;
   useful"), and her letter: "Do not let the punishment improve the Company
   in the telling."

The blue cotton (see below) is kept folded beneath the counter, not in the
fault. Joan inherits the whole arrangement and by 1635 has added nothing of
her own — "What she chooses to put there will belong to her time."

## Objects

- **The meat pie** — mutton and mace, baked on word of a ship. 1612: Tom eats
  a quarter, the rest goes to Jack. 1626: arrives before forgiveness, without
  ale. 1633: standing orders — "feed him first. Then tell him." 1635: Joan's
  version has thyme in the crust — "not imitation, inheritance."
- **The bung mallet** — Maggie's tool, never called a weapon; "in the hand of
  a woman who knows to an ounce what it can crack." Passed to Joan (darker
  from thirty years of use).
- **The blue cotton** — printed with a red climbing flower "no English loom
  has learned to make." Bought outside Surat, carried through monsoon and
  Portuguese attack. "Table cloth. / For which table? / One that doesn't have
  men cutting tar on it. / Then you have brought it to the wrong house." The
  exchange is replayed word for word with Joan in 1635 — she smiles; "Tom
  hears another voice and does not mistake one woman for the other." Tom
  meets the same pattern again in a Surat bale after Amboyna.
- **The genever stone bottle** — Hendricks's "Dutch physic." Passed to Tom
  (1603, courage to reach the clerk), to Maria (1603, courage to reach
  Amsterdam; 1625 she pours it for Tom with Hendricks's exact formula), and
  poured out on the Pelican's flagstones by Carter in 1626 — after which no
  one in the house asks for it by name for a generation.
- **The boatswain's whistle** — Tom's father's spare, worn thin by another
  man's thumb; the old one buried with him. "He made me promise I'd use it
  better. / Have you? / Most days."
- **Pieter's coat and book** — the coat given, not lent ("A given thing
  cannot be called back. You of all people should own one thing with that
  property"); the book's refrain "*Make another*" is the seed of Maria's
  whole duplicate doctrine.
- **The teak desk(s)** — no carving, tested with both palms, "too heavy to
  run with and too heavy for one man to take." Second desk to Aminah; the
  first crosses to Batavia and its string wall.
- **The covered cup** — Daniel's, always set on the left "so his hand can
  find it before his eyes must." The deck's mercy: "A deck, it turns out,
  has better manners than a court."
- **Barnaby's stool** — empty by unspoken treaty 1604–1612; Tom sits on it
  unasked and the room lets him. It is "the stool nearest the fire" for the
  rest of the book.
- **Maggie's rag / the wiped counter** — wiping the counter that does not
  need wiping is Maggie's tell for feeling; Joan inherits the gesture
  ("because the woman who taught her wiped it").

## Repeated sentences (use them exactly)

- "Food first." (Maggie; carried east by Tom; Iskandar's version: "Before. A
  dead broker reads nothing.")
- "That was not my question." (Maggie → Tom → repeated by Tom to boys and
  factors; the question behind it is always "Do you want the sea?")
- "There is always a *with or without me*." (Maggie, 1612; pre-echoed by
  Harcourt's "Another clerk will. But another clerk will have the place.")
- "One hand. One foot. Then the others." (Tom to Jack aloft; again after the
  flux; the template for "Can you walk that far? / Then that is all we are
  doing today" with Daniel.)
- "Find the join." (Tom's splice test; becomes Jack's craft creed — "Where I
  can see both ends of the rope.")
- "Make another." (Pieter's fever refrain → Maria's doctrine of the true
  copy: "A true copy is a second witness, and the second is harder to
  kill.")
- "Paper records power. It does not replace it." (Maria; Jack hears the
  lesson at the pay table "without knowing her.")
- "The ships go out. The boys sign." (Chapter 2's close; the Epilogue adds
  "The Pelican pours.")
- "Not love. Recognition." (Maria and Tom, 1603 and 1625 — the same look,
  twice, twenty-two years apart.)
- "Not pride. Entry." / "Not approval. Entry." (Maggie's and Joan's nods —
  bookkeeping as emotion.)
- "grief becomes use" (Maggie's diagnosis of Carter, 1612; the engine of the
  whole Company theme — Amboyna becomes cotton, warnings become
  qualifications.)

## Themes (what the book is arguing)

1. **A room can beat a bully; no room has ever stood up to a paper.**
   (Hendricks, Ch 2.) The 1603 trial is the one victory; everything after
   measures what paper does that fists cannot.
2. **A verdict is not the same as justice.** The room saves Maria AND lets
   Rook go; both accounts stay open for thirty years. Never let one cancel
   the other.
3. **The counter-ledger.** Official records are true documents built to bury
   truth (the coroner's finding, Daniel's deposition, the factor's margin).
   The answer is not protest but the true copy, kept where merchants do not
   think to search "because merchants do not believe taverns remember."
4. **Grief becomes use.** Every death is converted into trade within a
   week — and the people converting it are never villains, which is the
   point.
5. **The machine improves through its correctors.** Tom's fixes make the
   Company stronger; Arthur's clarity makes harm orderly; Maria's warning
   becomes a hiring qualification. Book One's tragedy of competence.
6. **Names against numbers.** Muster vs. names — Pell entered as cargo,
   Ellis/Brown/Cooper/Venn forced into the book, ten blank lines for the
   Japanese dead, Rafi's payment "owed," the notches. Keeping a name is not
   justice and is never called justice; it is what the living can reach.

## Voice and style (for any new or edited prose)

- **No quotation marks.** Dialogue sits in the prose unmarked; attribution
  by rhythm. Italics only for foreign words, ship names, written text and
  the occasional stressed phrase.
- **Ledger diction.** Feelings arrive as bookkeeping: entries, accounts,
  columns, rates, discharge, "the sum," "both accounts remain open."
  Characters "file," "enter," "audit" experience.
- **Short declaratives, paired truths.** "Both truths stand." "Both facts
  stand." The narrator constantly holds two things without resolving them.
- **Chapter shape:** epigraph blockquote (year + one quoted line + speaker),
  then Roman-numeral sections with short titles. Chapters close on a turned
  phrase, often a repeated formula.
- **Historical texture, invented people.** Real events (charter, first
  fleet, Swally, Amboyna, *Batavia* wreck, Pelsaert's tribunal, Loos &
  Pelgrom put ashore) anchor invented principals. The foreword insists: the
  people never existed; the history is real.
- **Understatement is the emotional register.** The biggest moments are an
  object moved six inches: a pint slid across a bar, a thimble left lying,
  a nod that is "entry."
