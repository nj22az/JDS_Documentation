# Proposed insert — "Tom at Surat" (letter-voice elevation)

## The principle (read this first)

The deployed §X "The Letter Not Sent" already fixes its own austere account of
the seven letters: a **blank page** with only her name, a **torn** page, the
**burned** three words, one "proper letter" **taken back** at the packet desk,
one **unsigned**. That conception — *a man who cannot write to her at all* — is
better than the planning doc's seven eloquent full letters, and it is already on
the page. So this pass does **not** import the eloquent letters. It lets the ache
speak *in Tom's voice at the instant he fails to send it*, honoring the deployed
austerity. Three small touches, one chapter, no new plot. The word "love"
appears in none of them.

---

## Beat 1 — §X, the first letter (mother's death)

**Why here.** The deployed first letter is a blank page. We keep the blank page.
We only let one true line get written before he sees it is a claim and destroys
it — so the reader feels the wound the summary currently states.

**Deployed text, exactly as it stands:**

> The first attempt begins in the Surat factory after his mother's parish letter
> arrives. He writes Maggie's name, then nothing beneath it. News of the death
> feels like a claim: feed me, forgive me, tell me I still belong somewhere. He
> folds the blank page around the name and puts it in his chest.

**Proposed:**

> The first attempt begins in the Surat factory after his mother's parish letter
> arrives. He writes Maggie's name. Then one line beneath it, because it is the
> truest thing in him and costs nothing to set down:
>
> *You would only put a plate in front of me and let me be quiet.*
>
> He looks at it a long time. Then he sees what it is — feed me, forgive me,
> tell me I still belong somewhere — a claim wearing grief's coat, addressed to
> a house that told him not to come back. He draws the pen through the line until
> the paper gives, folds the blank name inside the ruin of it, and puts it in
> his chest.

## Beat 2 — §IV, after Yusuf is cleared

**Why here.** The deployed chapter clears Yusuf of the false theft but never
lets Tom tie the save to Maggie in his own voice — the single clearest proof
that she is the instrument he now works by. This is **interior address, not a
written letter**, so it does not disturb §X's list (whose third letter is "the
debt was corrected"). It is the "penance aimed" idea, shown once, quietly.

**Deployed text, exactly as it stands (end of the Yusuf beat):**

> Tom makes the clerk cross out every line, write that the suspected theft arose
> from a copying error and put the factor's name beneath the correction. Yusuf
> watches from the wall. He does not thank Tom when released. Gratitude would
> accept that Tom had the right to decide whether the page lived.

**Proposed — add one paragraph immediately after:**

> Tom makes the clerk cross out every line, write that the suspected theft arose
> from a copying error and put the factor's name beneath the correction. Yusuf
> watches from the wall. He does not thank Tom when released. Gratitude would
> accept that Tom had the right to decide whether the page lived.
>
> He does not write it down — he has learned to the penny what writing her
> costs — but he says it to her the way he says everything now, on the middle
> watch, to a woman eleven thousand miles off who cannot hear it: *I knew to lay
> the one page against the other because you are in my head, standing at that bar
> asking whose name it is. You put yourself there. I did not invite you and I
> cannot get you out.* A man was carried east inside another without her leave,
> and it kept a rope off a third man's neck. It is the one part of her door he
> is glad of.

## Beat 3 — §X, the burned fifth

**Why here.** The deployed fifth already carries the self-diagnosis in the
narrator's summary ("appear humble until Tom reads the demand hidden inside
them"). Giving it the letter's own words sharpens the same beat — same letter,
same meaning, richer voice — and is the one place the planning doc and the
deployed reader already agree.

**Deployed text, exactly as it stands:**

> The fifth contains only the words *You were right*, which appear humble until
> Tom reads the demand hidden inside them: now tell me I am no longer wrong.
>
> He burns that one.

**Proposed:**

> The fifth contains only the words *You were right*, and he has been ten years
> getting to them. They look like the humblest thing a man ever set down, until
> he reads what stands under them: *a man does not say you were right to the one
> who put him out unless he means, underneath it, so let me come back.* It is a
> demand in an apology's coat — the neatest sum he ever built, and he built it
> against her, to turn his being right about his being wrong into a key for her
> door. She taught him to see that trick. She would see it in a heartbeat.
>
> He burns that one. It is the nearest thing to relief he has felt in ten years,
> which is exactly why he may not keep it.
