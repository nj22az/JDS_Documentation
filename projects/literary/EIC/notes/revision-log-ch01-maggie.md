## Context

The author's draft was largely a re-pitch of a scene **Ch1 already contains** (the thimble,
"Forty-three out of five hundred," Governor Smythe, "die for a clove," the maimed Red Dragon
crew, the mother-look, "Till you're back"). Only three things were genuinely new — and each
collided with canon, so each was put to the author as a fork.

## Author decisions (via clarify)

1. **Portrait** → *Weathered & formidable* (keep past-forty toughness; add "eyes blue-grey as a
   cold tide, missed nothing"; NO beauty-inventory — the book gives none).
2. **Hidden strength** → *Command, not combat* (no coiled-power/bare-handed beat — keeps Su's
   hidden-fighter identity singular).
3. **Past / the Swedish son** → *Son as the explicit Erik link.* Adopt the widowed-mother origin
   and make the Swedish son the on-page ancestral root of Erik Johansson (ch12/ch14).

## Reconciliation design

- Her first husband is **Johan** (Swedish fisherman, died of illness — pointedly NOT the sea).
  Their son → a **Johansson** → the name returns generations later as **Erik Johansson** (ch12).
- **Ashby becomes her second husband** (English Thames waterman, lost at sea off Africa), which
  also fixes a latent ordering wrinkle in the old text (Ashby was "first buried" yet "gone eleven
  years" while the sea-lost husband was "twelve years" — i.e. earlier).
- Arrival changed from "girl of 17, single" → "older widow starting over," so §VI's Hendricks
  memory ("young in much the same way") and §II's "quarter-century… at seventeen" both had to move.
