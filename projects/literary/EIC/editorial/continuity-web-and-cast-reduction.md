## 1. Authority stack and rules

Precedence when sources disagree:

1. The deployed chapters (the live reader; recovered text in the current
   bundle extraction).
2. The deployed appendices (`book-one-character-bible`,
   `appendix-1b-character-map`) — except where they contradict a chapter;
   §9 lists the known contradictions and their queued fixes.
3. Sibling-novel bibles (`kings-of-bengal/editorial/character-bible.md`,
   `watchmans-daughter/editorial/*`).

   working distillations, corrected by this document where the stale mirror
   misled them (Rook's fate, Juror 6, Mercer kinship, Joan).
5. Outline notes (`notes/outline-and-craft-plan.md`) — §3(b-i/ii)'s
   Gothenburg/younger-brother mechanism for the Johansson line is
   **superseded** by appendix-1b's "Maggie's son by Johan" mechanism.

Rules every wire in this document must pass:

- **The seasoning test.** Delete the connection: no scene may stop making
  sense. A wire is implementable as at most one surname, object, habit or
  future line of prose.
- **No new stations except by replacement.** A named thread may never
  generate a new prose beat unless it replaces a thinner passage. One
  authorized exception exists in this whole design: §12's names payoff.
- **Family-saga budget** (per outline §10): at most one new *stated* blood
  tie per volume; zero stated cross-volume blood ties, ever.
- **Standalone contract** (six-volume-omnibus-plan.md): no book may require
  another; the web is a reward for the omnibus reader, never an instruction
  for the standalone reader.

## 7. The cache — three-strata reconciliation

The 1635 epilogue is the authoritative Book One inventory; ch-2019 is the
authoritative excavation. The strata model reconciles both with
appendix-1b:

**Stratum 1 — the scored timber.** The notches (multiple blades across
centuries, per ch-2019's "more than one blade"); Tom's early notch; and —
proposed — Samuel Naylor's hooked N, scored into the hollow's timber

"left"): Bell's page (1603, Arthur's hand on the outside); the names/wager
sheet — "Tom … signed Thursday" — canonized as the counter-ledger's index,

cotton, entered by Joan — her one addition**. This answers 1635's "what
she chooses to put there will belong to her time" and pays off ch-2019's
photographed "corner of blue cloth."

**Stratum 3 — the four left testimonies** (each at the leaver's hardest
choice, unknown to each other, per appendix-1b): the thimble (1603),
Daniel's true account (1626), Nathaniel's testimony (1701), Gwen's
notebook (1940).

**2019 disposition map:** notebook → Bow cousin → borough archive;

blue-cloth corner and the unidentified soft papers (Bell / Daniel / Maria
— "illegible pending conservation") stay in the wall; encrypted photo sets

**Hard guard:** the illegible papers are **never identified on any future
page**. Naming Daniel's account in 2019 would hand Jerry the completeness

## 12. The Maria rework

Author-directed (2026-07-22), rulings locked: **fluent by arc's end** ·
**implicit heritage + one late payoff** · **loose homage**.

**Mara de Sousa becomes Maria de Sousa** — a woman concealing Japanese
heritage. A loose, fully fictionalized homage to the Lady Mariko figure of
Clavell's *Shōgun* (whose baptismal name is Maria): the shape is borrowed
— tainted lineage, devout convert, the translator as the bridge between
powers — the person, family and events are this book's own.

**Hidden backstory (bible-level only; never on any page):** a samurai-class
Japanese Christian family destroyed after her father moved against a
warlord; she survives because she had already been sent away — Nagasaki →
Macau (years in Macau = her native-grade Cantonese) → the Portuguese
captors' circuit, São Tomé to Macau, staked at cards, sold twice → de
Sousa, "the surname is her captors'." Baptized Maria by the Jesuits.
Concealment motives: the family's disgrace, Kirishitan persecution, and
the safety of never being an entry anyone can price twice. Period
anchoring is sound: the Portuguese carried Japanese slaves and converts
through Macau in exactly these years, and "Maria" is the standard
baptismal name of Japanese Christian women.

**Languages (the arc is preserved; zero chapters break):** 1603 =
Cantonese + Portuguese + concealed native Japanese. Dutch acquired
1603–1612 exactly as written (*hout* → the teak desk) to full fluency.
English self-taught and weakest — the deployed text already says her
English is "exact and slightly formal," which now reads as the accent of a
woman translating through her third acquired language. Working Malay.

**Implicit signals (full seasoning spec — nothing else):**
- She never speaks Japanese aloud, even in panic. The 1603 "panicked mix
  of Cantonese and Portuguese" becomes discipline, not limitation — the
  bible records it; no chapter states it.
- The 1630 packet addressed "in four hands that are really one" gains its
  unwritten fifth language as the bible's quietest note.

**The one late payoff — the single authorized new station in this whole
design:** the ten Japanese soldiers executed at Amboyna, for whom Tom's
private list keeps ten blank lines ("names not yet recovered… That is
what it is for"), are recovered **through Maria** — delivered without
explanation, preferred placement in the Batavia release material
(19-1625), alternatively one line in 18-1623. The room never learns why
she could get them; no page ever explains it. The blank lines the book
already left open are the reason this beat is authorized.

**Guards:** see §11.12. Additionally, the heritage never blurs into the
Zhang/Canton lineage — her concealed nation is Japan, and the Canton
practice belongs to the people the Company's later war displaced.

**Prose-pass footprint (inventory only; not executed in this pass):**
- Global rename Mara → Maria: **323 occurrences across 17 deployed pages**
  (01-1603 ×16, 02-1603 ×6, 04-1629 ×67, 05-1635 ×1, 06-1603 ×24,
  08-1604 ×77, 09-1612 ×48, 10-1623 ×39, 14-1614 ×1, 17-1622 ×2,
  19-1625 ×12, 20-1603 ×1, 21-1611 ×1, appendix-0 ×1, appendix-1b ×8,
  book-one-character-bible ×18, part-one ×1).
- Bell's ledger line becomes "Maria de Sousa — Portuguese servant —
  transfer pending."
- The two signal touches above; the names payoff.
- Scene-logic check: chapters 1, 3, 5, 7, 15, 20, 22 verified — no scene
  depends on her *not* being Japanese; the concealment is compatible with
  every existing beat, including the tribunal (she translates
  Dutch/English/Portuguese only, as written).
