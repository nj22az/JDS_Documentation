Appendix

Select Bibliography

The East India Company

Dalrymple, William. The Anarchy: The Relentless Rise of the East India Company. London: Bloomsbury, 2019.

Robins, Nick. The Corporation That Changed the World: How the East India Company Shaped the Modern Multinational. London: Pluto Press, 2006.

Keay, John. The Honourable Company: A History of the English East India Company. London: HarperCollins, 1991.

Mukherjee, Ramkrishna. The Rise and Fall of the East India Company. New York: Monthly Review Press, 1974.

Bowen, H.V. The Business of Empire: The East India Company and the Making of the Modern World. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2006.

India and Bengal

Sen, Amartya. Poverty and Famines: An Essay on Entitlement and Deprivation. Oxford: Clarendon Press, 1981.

Mukherjee, Madhusree. Churchill's Secret War: The British Empire and the Ravaging of India during World War II. New York: Basic Books, 2010.

Dirks, Nicholas B. The Scandal of Empire: India and the Creation of Imperial Britain. Cambridge: Harvard University Press, 2006.

Opium and China

Lovell, Julia. The Opium War: Drugs, Dreams and the Making of China. London: Picador, 2011.

Hanes, W. Travis III, and Frank Sanello. Opium Wars: The Addiction of One Empire and the Corruption of Another. New York: Sourcebooks, 2002.

London and the Thames

Ackroyd, Peter. London: The Biography. London: Chatto & Windus, 2000.

Mort, Frank. The East End: 400 Years of London Life. London: Chatto & Windus, 2020.

The Blitz and the Second World War

Calder, Angus. The People's War: Britain 1939–1945. London: Jonathan Cape, 1969.

Ziegler, Philip. London at War, 1939–1945. London: Hamish Hamilton, 1995.

Corporations and Empire

Micklethwait, John, and Adrian Wooldridge. The Company: A Short History of a Revolutionary Idea. New York: Modern Library, 2003.

Anderson, Matthew C. The Corporation under Siege, 1600–1860. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2023.

Suggested additions (for the new chapters — not yet sourced)

  • A source on the Hastings impeachment (1790 chapter thread) — P.J. Marshall's The Impeachment of Warren Hastings (1965) is the standard study; Dirks's The Scandal of Empire (already listed) centres on it.
  • A source on gin, quinine, and the gin & tonic (the gin thread) — Kim Walker and Mark Nesbitt's Just the Tonic: A Natural History of Tonic Water (Kew Publishing, 2019).
  • A source on the 1772 credit crisis, the bailout, and the Boston Tea Party (1774 chapter) — Benjamin Woods Labaree's The Boston Tea Party (1964) is the standard account; Nick Robins (already listed) covers the bailout.
  • A source on Henry Every, the Ganj-i-Sawai, and the first worldwide manhunt (1696 chapter) — Steven Johnson's Enemy of All Mankind (2020) is the standard modern account.
  • A source on Captain Kidd's trial and the missing French passes (1701 chapter).
  • A source on the Bounty mutiny and Bligh's open-boat voyage (1790 chapter) — Caroline Alexander's The Bounty (2003) is the standard modern account.
  • A source on the Cutty Sark's 1880 Smith/Francis killing and Captain Wallace's suicide (1880 chapter).
  • A source on the 1888 Whitechapel murders, for the factual scaffolding underneath the invented resolution (1888 chapter) — Paul Begg's Jack the Ripper: The Definitive History is a reasonable standard reference.
