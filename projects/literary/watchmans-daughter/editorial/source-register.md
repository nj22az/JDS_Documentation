All word counts include Markdown headings and metadata. Classification refers to
the prospective standalone book, not to the importance of the chapter within
*The Front-Row Seat*.

| 2019 — *What the Suit Didn't See* | 4,433 | Su and Erik's descendant | Optional coda; one paragraph in §VII is directly relevant |

The complete packet is 34,240 words. Only about half belongs directly to Su's
standalone narrative; the rest supplies history, motifs and alternative endings.
Copying all six chapters into one volume would preserve the anthology's episodic
shape rather than create a Su novel.

## Reference material also preserved

- the live Foreword;
- the Author's Note, including the Whitechapel disclaimer;
- the timeline;
- the internal character and thread map.

## Material deliberately excluded

- the 1603 Maggie/Tom chapters;
- the explicit Maggie-to-Johansson genealogy;
- unrelated Company chapters whose motifs merely touch the same river or tavern;
- website presentation code and compiled reader files.

## Provenance rule

Every copied source file retains its original `live-canon-source` comment. The
source packet therefore remains traceable even if the anthology changes later.
