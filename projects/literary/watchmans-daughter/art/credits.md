### Chapter One — The Copper

- **Filename:** `plates/01-the-copper.jpg`
- **Chapter:** One, *The Copper*
- **Generation date:** 12 July 2026
- **Tool path:** OpenAI built-in image generation; original historical scene,
  followed by one targeted equipment correction
- **Character references:** `reference/liz-su-laundry.png` and
  `reference/su-cray-alley.png` (Su only)
- **Final prompt:** Preserve Su, Sau-Ling, Wei, their poses and the pre-dawn
  laundry yard. Replace the implausible freestanding cylindrical tank with a
  broad Victorian wash copper permanently set into a squat brick furnace, with
  a coal firebox beneath, a plain wooden lid and a narrow seam leak running down
  the brickwork. Keep the plate vertical, painterly, sepia and blue-grey; no text,
  modern equipment, exotic décor, martial posing or horror.
- **Historical control:** Period coppers were commonly broad pans set into brick
  housings with a fire pit below. Reference object: [National Trust Collections,
  washing copper 28181](https://www.nationaltrustcollections.org.uk/object/28181).
- **Post-processing:** Accepted lossless RGB master retained separately; repository
  plate exported as an sRGB JPEG at quality 88, 1023 × 1537.
- **Notes:** The illustration deliberately presents a family solving a work
  problem. It is the visual standard for later original plates.
