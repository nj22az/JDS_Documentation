# Illustration Programme

The illustrations serve *The Watchman's Daughter* as a self-contained historical
novel. They show Su's work, family, friendships and inherited practice before they
show the alley. The visual sequence must not turn the book into a Ripper mystery
or imply that Su is investigating Dr Cray.

## Visual language

- Original narrative plates use restrained Victorian realism: painterly detail,
  muted sepia and umber colour, rain-muted blue-grey daylight and practical light
  from oil lamps or the wash fire.
- Plates are composed vertically for a printed page. Important faces and hands
  stay clear of the gutter and trim.
- Su remains consistent with the established live-edition artwork: nineteen,
  Chinese-English, oval face, pale complexion, dark almond-shaped eyes, small
  straight nose, restrained mouth, long black hair in two braids, practical dark
  work dress and apron. Her expression is calm and private, never vacant.
- The Zhangs' home is working, cramped and ordinary. Avoid exotic décor, opium-den
  imagery, theatrical martial-arts poses and anachronistic clothing or equipment.
- Violence is shown through interrupted space, balance, hands and consequence.
  No gore, glamour or triumphant poses.
- Generated art contains no title lettering, captions, signatures or watermarks.
  Typography is added later in the book layout.

## Later chapters

Illustrations for Chapters Eleven through Twenty-Five and the epilogue are chosen
only after the prose exists. Reserve these indispensable images:

- Liz and Su at the laundry in *Words with Floors*;
- the open ledger column after the double event;
- Su carrying ordinary linen on the last Monday;
- the unnamed woman escaping while Su drops the basket;
- Su refusing the physician's case clasp;
- Wei correcting Su at dawn after the alley;
- Erik's salt-stiff shirts on the counter in 1892.

The alley may receive two plates at most. Cray is never placed beside any of the
Whitechapel victims, and the physician's case is never shown open.

## Production controls

- Keep a character reference image for Su attached to every generation request.
- Generate each distinct scene separately; do not ask one prompt for a montage.
- Inspect hands, clothing, tools, signs and architecture before accepting a plate.
- Retain lossless generated masters locally and in the generation history. Save
  repository-sized, colour-checked publication previews in `art/plates/`.
- Record prompt, source and credit information in `credits.md`.
