# Change Log

## 2026-07-13 — The Prospect restored as stage

- Rebuilt all twenty-two chapters around the Prospect of Whitby as the fixed
  dramatic stage.
- Reclassified the quay, foreshore, warehouses and yards as the stage's immediate
  wings; all decisive turns now return to the tavern.
- Replaced independent Bengal scenes with testimony, sustained Sufiya letters,
  cargo evidence and owned memory.
- Added a stage map for the third stool, bar, fire, river window, door, back room
  and hidden Cache.
- Reframed Chapter One to open inside the Prospect with the failed pump leather,
  Rahim, Elias and Coates already sharing the room.

## 2026-07-13 — Cornish engineering history

- Rebuilt the engineering character as Elias Tregowan, an English shipwright and
  pump-maker born at Saltash, Cornwall, in 1735.
- Removed the Swedish biography and name from the dossier and opening chapter.
- Established a historically compatible professional acquaintance with William
  Bligh: *Resolution* at Deptford in 1776 and *Bounty*'s breadfruit conversion
  there in 1787.
- Made the Bligh connection useful but limited. Elias knows his competence and
  working manner, not the unknowable cause of the mutiny.
- Connected Elias's work on *Bounty*'s plant-cabin drainage to the novel's larger
  question about technically correct work serving a purpose left unexamined.

## 2026-07-13 — Opening chapter

- Drafted Chapter One, “The Notebook Man.”
- Introduced Elias Tregowan through a chain-pump diagnosis shared with Rahim Ali.
- Connected the engineering theme—an incomplete test producing a correct but
  misleading result—to Samuel Naylor's first compromised cargo account.
- Brought Samuel, Nell Finch, Sergeant Coates, Rahim and Aldridge into the same
  opening movement without altering the protected live-canon chapter.
- Corrected the engineering worksheet to distinguish chain-pump leathers from
  valve-pump components.

## 2026-07-13 — Development project opened

- Created the standalone *Kings of Bengal* development project from the
  protected 1757, 1770, 1774 and 1790 anthology units.
- Established Samuel Naylor as the recurring Wapping protagonist and identified
  him with the existing unnamed notebook man.
- Added Rahim Ali, Sufiya Ali, Nell Finch, Bess Naylor and Edmund Aldridge as the
  new governing ensemble.
- Added Elias Tregowan, an original eighteenth-century shipwright and pump-maker
  inspired by the practical engineering appeal of Jake Holman without importing
  the copyrighted character or the 1920s setting of *The Sand Pebbles*.
- Added an engineering-experience worksheet so the author's merchant-marine and
  field-service incidents can be translated into Elias's period-accurate scenes.
- Defined a five-part architecture, historical guardrails and the first expanded
  chapter draft.
