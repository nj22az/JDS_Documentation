# Canon and Guardrails

## Protected seed material

The following live-canon units supply the fixed historical and thematic spine:

- 1757 — *A Soldier's Arithmetic*: Coates's testimony, the fixed battle, the
  mango grove and the Company's passage from merchant to territorial power.
- 1770 — *What Mulvey Saw*: famine, Mulvey's return, Aldridge's composure and the
  human cost excluded from London accounts.
- 1774 — *Too Big to Sink*: insolvency, government rescue, the Tea Act, Boston
  and Naylor's hooked mark.
- 1790 — *Forty-Seven Days*: Alec Munro, Bligh's open-boat voyage, Hastings's
  trial, breadfruit and the fuchsia.

The standalone may expand, rearrange and deepen this material but must not
silently contradict the live anthology. Proposed changes remain here until the
author approves them.

## Binding story principles

1. **Bengal has interior life.** Bengali characters do not exist merely to
   authenticate English guilt, explain famine or die beautifully.
2. **The Prospect remains a witness, not a magical institution.** Information
   arrives through ships, work, letters, newspapers and people.
3. **No lecture characters.** Every historical explanation must cost, protect,
   tempt or endanger the speaker.
4. **Correct numbers may still lie.** The book's central image is an account
   whose arithmetic is exact and whose categories conceal the crime.
5. **The Company is not the Crown.** It acts under charter, exercises delegated
   and acquired powers, and is regulated and rescued by government; formal Crown
   rule lies later, in 1858.
6. **No simple English innocence.** Samuel's ignorance is real at nineteen, but
   it does not remain an excuse once his livelihood depends on the system.
7. **No simple Bengali victimhood.** Sufiya and Rahim work, bargain, withhold,
   misjudge, love and pursue futures beyond surviving the Company's violence.
8. **The famine is not spectacle.** Show entitlement, prices, grain movement,
   tax demands, migration and household decisions. Do not aestheticise bodies.
9. **The Cache is optional continuity.** Its origin enriches the omnibus but the
   novel resolves without knowledge of its later discovery.
10. **The fuchsia remains the counter-image.** It crosses an ocean through care
    rather than conquest and ends the book with living inheritance.

## Historical boundaries requiring verification before final prose

- Exact Company offices, warehouse procedures and tally terminology in Wapping.
- Bengali river occupations, shipboard ranks and language use in the 1750s–90s.
- Revenue collection and grain-market chronology before and during the 1770
  famine.
- The timing and mechanism of the 1772 crisis, the 1773 Regulating Act and the
  Tea Act.
- News travel from Bengal, Boston and the *Bounty* to London.
- Warren Hastings impeachment dates and what a Wapping reader could know in
  March 1790.

## Character exclusions

- Rahim never speaks generic broken English for atmosphere.
- Sufiya is not Samuel's moral tutor, distant love interest or symbolic victim.
- Nell is not merely the woman who keeps the bar while men discuss history.
- Bess does not inherit perfect courage or complete knowledge from her parents.
- Aldridge is not secretly responsible for every event. He represents ordinary,
  rewarded administrative choices rather than a single master conspiracy.
- Real Bengali suffering is never invented merely to motivate Samuel.
- Elias Tregowan is not Jake Holman moved into another century. He has an original
  biography, relationships, voice and outcome; the shared appeal is engineering
  competence in conflict with hierarchy.
- Elias's acquaintance with William Bligh is professional and limited. It may
  complicate the public caricature of Bligh but cannot make Elias an invented
  confidant, secret cause of the voyage or final judge of the mutiny.
- Engineering is never decorative jargon. Every technical detail must change a
  decision, expose a concealed cause or reveal character.
