# Character Bible

## Governing ensemble

### Samuel Naylor

- Born Wapping, 1738; nineteen when the Plassey news reaches London.
- Son of a dead sailmaker and a mother who takes warehouse sewing.
- Excellent at weights, marks and columns; initially believes accuracy and truth
  are the same virtue.
- The unnamed notebook man from *A Soldier's Arithmetic*. He is sent by reforming
  MP Nathaniel Hollis to take Coates's statement.
- Hollis suppresses the testimony once it threatens allies and investments.
- Samuel later accepts Company warehouse work because it pays reliably. This is
  not hypocrisy in one leap; it is a chain of defensible decisions.
- Uses a hooked **N** to identify totals he has checked personally. The mark later
  appears on tea chests and in the Cache.
- Marries Nell Finch. Their intimacy is practical, argumentative and durable.
- Central fault: he records what happened but repeatedly postpones deciding what
  the record requires him to do.
- Arc: faith in correct accounts → knowledge of omitted categories → compromise
  → attempted disclosure → acceptance that preservation may be the only honest
  act still available to him.

### Eleanor “Nell” Finch

- Born 1736; works at the Prospect beside her elder brother William, the current
  leaseholder.
- Descended implicitly from Esther Finch's keeper line; the genealogy is never
  delivered as exposition.
- Reads rooms, credit and appetite faster than Samuel reads numbers.
- Keeps a private slate of meals given without payment and refuses to call it
  charity. She calls it loss and chooses it deliberately.
- Marries Samuel without surrendering the tavern or becoming subordinate to his
  plot.
- Central fault: she is so skilled at making survival look ordinary that her
  family sometimes mistakes endurance for consent.
- Arc: observer of Samuel's accounts → active protector of Rahim → co-author of
  the hidden record → mother forced to let Bess make a riskier choice.

### Rahim Ali

- Born near Hugli, 1739, into a Muslim river family that pilots, loads and
  provisions vessels.
- Learns Bengali and Persian account hands from his father and shipboard English,
  Hindustani and Portuguese vocabulary through work.
- Begins as a boatman's apprentice; later sails as a lascar and becomes a
  seacunny, trusted with steering and position.
- First crosses Samuel's path through a cargo mark in 1757; reaches Wapping in
  person during the famine year.
- Precise, dryly funny and unwilling to perform gratitude for ordinary decency.
- Wants command, wages paid in full and the ability to return home on his own
  terms—not settlement in England as a narrative reward.
- Central fault: he withholds information to preserve control, sometimes after
  secrecy has stopped protecting anyone.
- Arc: local river knowledge used by Company shipping → oceanic skill without
  equal status → abandoned witness in London → partner in a transoceanic record
  → serang who chooses what and whom to carry home.

### Sufiya Ali

- Born near Hugli, 1733; Rahim's elder sister.
- Literate in Bengali and working Persian because the family river business
  cannot survive on memory alone.
- Widowed before 1757 and takes control of two cargo boats whose ownership is
  disputed by male relatives and Company contractors.
- Knows markets through rice, oil, salt, cloth, boat hire and household credit,
  not through abstract imperial policy.
- Her letters and account copies travel irregularly through Rahim and trusted
  crews. Some arrive years late; some never arrive.
- Central fault: she treats every relationship as an account she can carry alone.
- Arc: protects the family boats → bargains with new Company power → keeps grain
  moving during scarcity → refuses a profitable contract that would empty local
  stores → rebuilds on a smaller scale after famine.

### Elizabeth “Bess” Naylor

- Born 1760 to Samuel and Nell.
- Fourteen during the Company crisis and thirty when the *Bounty* news arrives.
- Grows up between the Prospect slate, warehouse marks and Rahim's river terms.
- Can recognise her father's hooked N before she can explain what it certifies.
- Works at the Prospect but is not destined automatically to inherit it.
- Helps Alec Munro search incomplete crew and shipping records in 1790.
- Central fault: she believes disclosure is inherently braver than concealment
  and initially underestimates what public truth can do to vulnerable people.
- Arc: curious child → dangerous reader of adult omissions → keeper of the hidden
  papers → author of the final note explaining why they were preserved.

### Edmund Aldridge

- Born 1726; Company warehouse examiner and later a minor proprietor and
  administrative adviser.
- Values solvency, continuity and legible procedure. He does not think of himself
  as cruel.
- Gives Samuel his first secure post and repeatedly protects him from consequences
  that Aldridge himself helped create.
- Believes an institution cannot be judged by every misery adjacent to its work.
- Central contradiction: personally generous, structurally ruthless.
- Never confesses, sneers at suffering or reveals a secret plan. His danger is
  that his reasoning is calm, useful and rewarded.

### Elias Tregowan

- Born at Saltash, Cornwall, in 1735; son of a shipwright working the Hamoaze and
  a mother who keeps household and piecework accounts for two related families.
- Apprentices in ship and mill work around Plymouth Sound. He goes to the Thames
  because Deptford and the private river yards offer mechanisms and vessels on a
  scale unavailable to an unknown journeyman at home.
- Reaches Wapping before 1757 and works between Deptford, river yards and Company vessels
  on chain pumps, elm pump barrels, leather valves, capstans, steering gear,
  blocks, windlasses and emergency hull repairs.
- Cornish without being written in phonetic dialect. Distance from Cornwall is
  part of his biography, not a running joke or mystical inheritance.
- Meets William Bligh professionally at Deptford in 1776 while *Resolution* is
  fitted for Cook's third voyage. Elias works on pumps and water-handling details;
  the twenty-two-year-old sailing master asks exact questions, remembers every
  answer and has no patience for an assurance that cannot be measured.
- Works on the 1787 conversion of *Bounty*, particularly the water and drainage
  arrangements around the breadfruit plant cabin. Bligh remembers him. They are
  neither close friends nor enemies: each respects the other's competence, and
  each finds the other's certainty exhausting.
- This connection makes Elias a witness to the vessel and to Bligh before the
  mutiny, not an authority on what happened in the Pacific. In 1790 he refuses
  both the tavern's monster and hero versions: he knows the man, but was not on
  the voyage.
- Believes machinery is preferable to institutions because a failed component
  leaves physical evidence. The novel spends thirty-three years disproving the
  comforting half of that belief: evidence matters only if someone is willing to
  name the cause.
- Keeps removed failed parts rather than trophies: a split valve leather, a
  twisted pin, a scored bearing surface, a bolt filed to hide lost diameter.
- Does not love machines more than people. He trusts machines because they do not
  require him to guess which politeness conceals the danger.
- Friendship with Rahim begins in professional disagreement. Rahim knows how a
  pump behaves at sea after three weeks of bad water; Elias knows how it should
  measure at the dock. Both are right, and the repair works only when each stops
  treating the other's knowledge as anecdote.
- Nell appreciates that he repairs before explaining. Samuel resents that Elias
  can identify a physical cause while an account can distribute responsibility
  until it disappears.
- Central fault: competence becomes refuge. If he can restore the mechanism, he
  tells himself the wider purpose of the vessel is not his department.
- Arc: solitary repairer → respected yard specialist → compromised certifier of
  deferred work → witness who names preventable failure → teacher who accepts
  that knowledge must be passed on, not guarded as personal leverage.
- By 1790, at fifty-five, his hands are stiff and two fingertips lack feeling.
  Bess records his diagnostic method while a younger apprentice performs the
  physical work.

#### Elias's diagnostic method

1. Ask what changed immediately before the fault.
2. Listen to the operator before touching the mechanism.
3. Separate symptom, failed part and initiating cause.
4. Measure the removed piece; do not trust the replacement label.
5. Restore function only after identifying what will make it fail again.
6. Write the cause plainly enough that a manager cannot rename it as weather,
   wear or operator error.

This method is the bridge for the author's own engineering experience. Its logic
may be retained even when the exact modern machine is translated into pumps,
rigging, steering gear, cargo handling or hull work.

## Existing seed characters

### Sergeant Coates

The first witness. His testimony gives Samuel the phrase “a transaction, with a
musket, in the rain.” He is neither spotless whistleblower nor brute: Company
pension, drink, shame and pride all remain active.

### Patrick Mulvey

Samuel's boyhood acquaintance, later a Company clerk in Bengal. His 1770 return
is therefore not a stranger's monologue but the collapse of someone Samuel once
envied for escaping Wapping.

### Alec Munro

The emotional focus of the 1790 movement. Bess helps him discover that record
systems list allegiances and outcomes more readily than frightened younger
brothers.

### William Finch

Nell's elder brother and the broad, permanently angry landlord from the 1757
seed chapter. His anger has a material history in the Spitalfields weaving trade.
He is difficult, not villainous, and his prohibition on gin remains binding.
