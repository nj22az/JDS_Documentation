specific date, sum, title, route and administrative mechanism must be checked
against a cited source before publication.

## Bengali and South Asian seafarers

The Company employed South Asian seamen from the seventeenth century and used
increasing numbers as territorial control and shipping expanded. “Lascar” was a
broad European category, not an ethnicity or a complete identity. Shipboard
ranks included serang and seacunny; their precise eighteenth-century duties and
spelling require checking in period muster and wage records.

Useful starting points:

- Royal Museums Greenwich, “Lascars and the East India Company”:

### Fictional-use decision

Rahim can plausibly work from the Hugli river system onto Company shipping and
reach London. His exact rank progression, articles, pay, clothing, accommodation
and return options remain provisional. The story must distinguish his own name,
languages and river community from the Company's collective label.

## London anchor

The Wapping material should be built from work:

- tally marks, warehouse warrants, damaged cargo and disputed weights;
- ship arrival intelligence reaching the tavern before official summaries;
- sailors waiting for pay or return berths;
- women extending, refusing and recording household credit;
- the contrast between physical goods on the Thames and abstractions in
  Leadenhall Street accounts.

Research required: which East India goods were unloaded where in 1757–90, the
legal boundaries of Company warehousing, tally-clerk employment and whether the
specific cargo marks used in the draft match period practice.

## Eighteenth-century engineering anchor

Jonas's work must remain appropriate to wooden sailing ships. He is not a marine
engineer in the later steam sense. His systems are mechanical and hydraulic:
chain and common pumps, valves and leathers, blocks, tackles, capstans,
windlasses, steering gear, hull fastenings, caulking, water casks, gun tackles
and workshop jigs.

Before drafting each repair, verify the particular mechanism against a period
shipbuilding or seamanship source. Modern diagnostic logic may inform Jonas's
thinking, but modern terms such as root-cause analysis, calibration certificate,
preventive maintenance and operator error must be translated into period action
and speech.

## Bengal anchor

Sufiya's scenes must treat the famine through changing choices rather than a
single retrospective number:

- grain price and availability;
- boat hire and confiscation;
- revenue demands;
- seed grain versus food;
- household credit and migration;
- contractors moving grain toward buyers with greater purchasing power.

The novel must not claim every boat of grain was directly ordered away by one
Company officer. Extraction emerges from revenue pressure, market power,
monopoly, official decisions and private profit acting together.

## Language

- Samuel and Nell use period English without heavy phonetic dialect.
- Rahim's English changes with exposure and context; it is never a stable comic
  marker. His shipboard vocabulary may be better than his tavern vocabulary.
- Sufiya's Bengali/Persian correspondence is rendered in clear English with the
  original form indicated only where the distinction matters.
- Avoid modern institutional phrases in dialogue, even when the narration
  explains the underlying idea plainly.
