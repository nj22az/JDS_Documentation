# The Prospect as Stage

## Dramatic geography

| Zone | Recurring use | Historical function |
|---|---|---|
| Third stool | Coates, Mulvey, Rahim and later Alec occupy the witness position | A person arrives carrying what official history excluded |
| Bar top | Slate, notebook, pump leather, cargo paper, tea mark and launch calculation | Unlike a desk, it forces different kinds of evidence into contact |
| Fire | Clothing dries, food is offered, letters are opened | Distant policy becomes bodily need |
| River window | Arrivals are seen before their meaning is known | The Thames supplies movement, delay and incomplete information |
| Door | Entrances, interruptions, creditors and returning sailors | History changes the room by arriving as a person |
| Back room | copying, private disagreement, household decisions | Public testimony becomes dangerous private record |
| Fabric of the building | first hidden packets and later Cache | The Prospect becomes a material archive |
| Stairs and foreshore | brief work, pursuit or river access | The stage's wings; action may leave the room without abandoning it |

## Repeated stage actions

The book should create continuity through repeated physical actions whose
meaning changes over thirty-three years:

1. Nell puts a cloth beneath Samuel's clean notebook.
2. A witness receives food or drink before being asked to perform testimony.
3. An object is placed on the bar and contradicts a written account.
4. Someone looks through the river window while deciding what can be said.
5. The public bar closes, but the real work continues.
6. A paper disappears into the building and returns years later.

## Memory rule

A memory sequence must answer four questions:

1. Who in the Prospect owns this memory?
2. What prompted it now?
3. What could that person actually have witnessed?
4. What changes in the room because it was told?

If the memory supplies background but changes no relationship, decision or
interpretation on stage, it belongs in research notes rather than the novel.

## Sufiya's presence

Sufiya never enters the Prospect physically, but she is not an absent symbol.
Her sustained letters create a second speaking position inside the room. The
novel may render a translated letter as direct first-person prose for several
pages, provided the material condition of the letter remains visible: damaged
paper, delayed arrival, missing sheets, a disputed translation or a recipient
choosing whether to read a private line aloud.

The English characters may respond to her account. They may not complete it for
her.
