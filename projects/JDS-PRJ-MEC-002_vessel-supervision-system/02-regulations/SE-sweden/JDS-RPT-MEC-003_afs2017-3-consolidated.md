## 1. Regulation Overview

### 1.1 Base Regulation

**AFS 2017:3** — "Use and Inspection of Pressurised Equipment" — entered into force 1 December 2017, replacing AFS 2005:3. It governs the use, supervision, and periodic inspection of pressurised equipment throughout its service life.

### 1.2 Amendments

| Amendment | Entered Into Force | Key Changes |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **AFS 2019:1** | 1 August 2019 | Clarified scope for piping systems; refined provisions for interval extensions; updated references to EU harmonised standards |
| **AFS 2020:10** | 1 November 2020 | Temporary provisions for extended inspection deadlines during extraordinary circumstances; acceptance of digital documentation and remote verification where physical access is restricted |
| **AFS 2022:2** | 1 June 2022 | Revised competence requirements for persons performing supervision and own inspections; updated classification thresholds for certain equipment categories; clarified provisions for equipment modifications |

## 2. Scope of Application

### 2.1 Equipment in Scope

The regulation applies to pressurised equipment where:
- Maximum allowable pressure (PS) exceeds **0.5 bar** above atmospheric, AND
- The pressure-volume product (PS x V) exceeds the lower threshold for classification

This includes:
- **Pressure vessels** — closed vessels designed to hold pressurised fluid
- **Piping** — pipe systems with components under pressure (as clarified by AFS 2019:1)
- **Safety accessories** — devices protecting pressurised equipment (safety valves, rupture discs, pressure switches)

### 2.2 Equipment Not in Scope

- Equipment below the PS x V threshold (< 50 bar-L for Group 2 gases)
- Transport equipment (covered by ADR/RID)
- Nuclear pressure equipment
- Equipment on ships (covered by maritime regulations)

## 3. Classification

### 3.1 Risk Classes

Pressurised equipment is classified into risk classes based on the pressure-volume product and the nature of the medium:

| Risk Class | Description | Inspection Regime |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Class A** | Higher-risk equipment: large PS x V product, dangerous media, or high consequences | Mandatory inspection by accredited body |
| **Class B** | Lower-risk equipment: moderate PS x V product, non-dangerous media | Accredited body or own inspection (with documented competence) |

### 3.2 Classification Thresholds — Group 2 Gases

| Class | PS x V (bar-L) | Typical Examples |
|-------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Class A** | > 10,000 | Large air receivers, industrial compressor tanks |
| **Class B** | 1,000 - 10,000 | Medium air receivers, hydraulic accumulators |
| Below B | 200 - 1,000 | Small vessels (reduced or no periodic inspection) |
| Simple PV | 50 - 200 | Small expansion tanks |
| Not classified | < 50 | Below regulatory threshold |

### 3.3 Classification Thresholds — Group 1 Gases

Thresholds are lower (more strict) for Group 1 (dangerous) media:

| Class | PS x V (bar-L) | Note |
|-------|----------------|------|
| **Class A** | > 3,000 | Lower threshold due to hazardous medium |
| **Class B** | 200 - 3,000 | Wider range captured |

### 3.4 Medium Groups

| Group | Definition | Examples |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Group 1** | Dangerous fluids: explosive, extremely flammable, flammable, very toxic, toxic, oxidising | LPG, ammonia, hydrogen, chlorine, acetylene |
| **Group 2** | All other fluids | Compressed air, nitrogen, water, steam, CO2, noble gases |

> The fluid group classification follows CLP Regulation (EC) 1272/2008, as referenced by PED 2014/68/EU. AFS 2022:2 updated certain classification thresholds to align with revised EU harmonised standards.

## 4. Ongoing Supervision Requirements

This is the core section relevant to the Vessel Supervision System.

### 4.1 General Obligation (Chapter 2, Section 3)

The operator shall ensure that pressurised equipment is subject to **ongoing supervision**. This supervision shall be:
- **Regular** — performed at defined intervals
- **Documented** — records kept for the life of the equipment
- **Adapted** — to the equipment type, operating conditions, and risk level

### 4.2 Supervision Program (Chapter 2, Section 3a)

The operator shall have a **documented supervision program** that describes:

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| **Equipment covered** | Every vessel, piping system, and safety accessory in scope |
| **Checks to perform** | Visual inspection, gauge readings, leak detection, safety device checks |
| **Intervals** | How often each check is performed (daily, weekly, monthly, etc.) |
| **Personnel** | Who performs the checks, and what competence they need |
| **Documentation** | How results are recorded and stored |
| **Findings management** | How deviations are handled, escalated, and closed |
| **Review frequency** | When the program itself is reviewed and updated |

### 4.3 Competence for Supervision (Chapter 2, Section 4)

As revised by AFS 2022:2:

Personnel performing ongoing supervision shall have:
- Knowledge of the hazards associated with pressurised equipment
- Understanding of the specific equipment and its operating conditions
- Ability to identify deviations from normal condition
- Knowledge of emergency and escalation procedures

Competence shall be **documented** and **maintained** (refresher training at defined intervals).

### 4.4 Equipment Register (Chapter 2, Section 5)

The operator shall maintain a register containing:

1. Identification of each piece of equipment (tag, serial number, description)
2. Location
3. Classification (risk class, PED category, medium group)
4. Inspection status (last inspection, next due, result)
5. Safety devices assigned to each vessel

### 4.5 Marking and Legibility (Chapter 2, Section 6)

Equipment markings (nameplates) shall be kept legible and accessible. If the original marking is damaged, the operator shall ensure replacement or supplementary marking.

### 4.6 Acting on Findings (Chapter 2, Section 7)

Deficiencies identified during supervision or inspection shall be remedied before the equipment is returned to or continued in service.

### 4.7 Incident Reporting (Chapter 2, Section 8)

The operator has a duty to report to Arbetsmiljöverket:
- Overpressure events
- Equipment failures
- Dangerous occurrences involving pressurised equipment

## 5. Periodic Inspection Requirements

### 5.1 Inspection Types

| Type | What It Involves |
|------|-----------------|
| **External examination** | Visual inspection of the exterior surface, connections, supports, safety devices |
| **Internal examination** | Inspection of the interior surface — requires depressurisation, draining, cleaning, and confined space entry |
| **Pressure test** | Hydrostatic (preferred) or pneumatic proof test to verify structural integrity |

### 5.2 Inspection Intervals

| Risk Class | External | Internal | Pressure Test |
|-----------|----------|----------|--------------|
| **Class A** | 24 months | 72 months | 144 months |
| **Class B** | 36 months | 72 months | — |

> AFS 2019:1 refined provisions for interval extensions where the operator can demonstrate favourable operating conditions and history. AFS 2020:10 introduced temporary provisions allowing extensions during extraordinary circumstances (e.g., pandemic restrictions), subject to risk assessment and authority notification.

### 5.3 Who May Inspect

| Risk Class | Inspection Type | Who |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Class A** | All types | Accredited inspection body (SWEDAC accredited) |
| **Class B** | External | Accredited body OR own inspection (documented competence) |
| **Class B** | Internal | Accredited inspection body |

AFS 2022:2 clarified the competence requirements for own inspection: the operator must demonstrate that the person performing the inspection has relevant technical education or equivalent experience, and has received specific training on the inspection methods used.

### 5.4 Inspection Results

| Result | Meaning | Required Action |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| **Approved** | Equipment is safe for continued service | Update register, file certificate |
| **Approved with remarks** | Equipment may continue with conditions | Address remarks within stated deadline, monitor |
| **Rejected** | Equipment is not safe for service | Take out of service immediately, do not return until re-inspected and approved |

## 6. Safety Devices

### 6.1 Requirements

Safety devices shall be:
- **Correctly configured** — set pressure shall not exceed the vessel's design pressure
- **Tested regularly** — at defined intervals (typically annually for safety valves)
- **Documented** — every test recorded with date, set pressure, lift pressure, and result
- **Not defeated** — safety valves shall not be gagged, blocked, or isolated without authorised procedures

### 6.2 Test Methods

| Device Type | Test Method | Typical Interval |
|------------|------------|-----------------|
| Safety valve | Lift test (on-line or bench) | 12 months |
| Rupture disc | Visual inspection, replace per manufacturer schedule | Per manufacturer |
| Pressure switch | Function test (verify trip point) | 12 months |

---

## 7. Documentation and Records

### 7.1 Retention Requirements

Records shall be kept **for the life of the equipment plus 5 years** after decommissioning. This includes:
- Equipment register entries
- Supervision round records
- Inspection certificates
- Safety device test records
- Corrective action records
- EU Declarations of Conformity

### 7.2 Digital Documentation

AFS 2020:10 explicitly accepted digital documentation and electronic records provided:
- Records are protected against unauthorised modification
- Records are backed up and recoverable
- Records can be presented to the authority on demand

> JDS uses Git version control, which inherently satisfies the integrity and traceability requirements for digital records.
