## 1. Purpose

To determine how *The Garage Reset* (JDS-PRJ-GEN-001) can earn as much as possible on
Amazon KDP, and to set the pricing, format, and launch configuration for the shippable
package. All figures are amazon.com USD rates current as of the June 2025 KDP changes.

## 2. The KDP economics (verified rates)

**eBook.** 70 % royalty for list prices $2.99–$9.99, minus a delivery fee of $0.15/MB
(our EPUB is well under 1 MB, so the fee is pennies). Outside that band the rate drops
to 35 %. The eBook must be priced at least 20 % below the print edition.

**Paperback (B&W interior).** Printing cost = **$0.85 + $0.012 x pages**. Royalty =
list price x 60 % (for books ≥ $9.99; 50 % below that) minus printing. The interior we
ship is **115 pages**, so printing = $0.85 + 115 x $0.012 = **$2.23/copy**.

**Kindle Unlimited (KDP Select).** ~$0.0045 per KENP page read from the monthly fund;
a full read of this book is roughly 170 KENP ≈ **$0.75–0.80**. Select requires eBook
exclusivity to Amazon for 90-day terms.

## 3. Pricing decision

| Format | List price | Per-copy royalty | Why |
|--------|-----------|------------------|-----|
| Kindle eBook | **$6.99** | **≈ $4.86** | Inside the 70 % band; impulse price; ≥20 % under print |
| Paperback 5.5x8.5, 115 pp | **$14.99** | **≈ $6.76** | Above the $9.99 threshold → 60 % tier; category-typical price |
| (launch week promo) | $0.99 eBook | ≈ $0.35 | Volume + rank + reviews, then raise to $6.99 |

Break-even on a typical $200–400 self-publishing outlay (proof copies, maybe a cover
designer) is **40–80 sales** — low risk.

## 4. The profit levers (what this plan actually changes)

1. **Ship both formats from day one.** The paperback earns more per copy *and* anchors
   the eBook's perceived value ("$14.99 → $6.99" reads as a deal). This category (home
   how-to) skews print-friendly.
2. **Keep the interior in the cheap tier.** B&W ink, standard 5.5x8.5 trim, 115 pages:
   every avoided 10 pages saves $0.12/copy forever. The line-art diagrams are grayscale
   *by design* — a color interior would roughly triple printing cost.
3. **Price the paperback ≥ $9.99.** The June 2025 tiering makes this a cliff: at $9.99+
   the rate is 60 %, below it 50 %. $14.99 is comfortably inside the category norm
   ($12.99–$16.99).
4. **Enroll in KDP Select for the first 90 days.** KU readers of practical non-fiction
   are real money (~$0.78/full read), and Select unlocks Countdown Deals / Free days
   for launch. Re-evaluate exclusivity after two terms.
5. **Diagrams double as A+ content.** The zone map, shadow board, and 10-step strip go
   into the listing's A+ modules — visual proof-of-method, which lifts conversion.
6. **Series economics.** Back matter plugs the next titles (*The Shed Reset*, storage
   locker spin-off…). Series pages on Amazon cross-sell automatically; book 2 makes
   book 1 more profitable retroactively.
7. **Second market for free.** The Swedish edition (*Städa i Garaget*) ships from the
   same toolchain to amazon.se, where the niche research showed **no competing book at
   all**. Different marketplace, same fixed costs already paid.
8. **Reviews before rank.** 10–15 beta readers with the EPUB, honest reviews on launch
   week. Conversion with 10+ reviews is a different business than with zero.

## 5. Revenue scenarios (eBook $6.99 / paperback $14.99, EN only)

| Scenario | Sales/month (e / p / KU reads) | Monthly royalty |
|----------|-------------------------------|-----------------|
| Quiet niche | 15 / 10 / 10 | ≈ $148 |
| Healthy niche | 50 / 35 / 40 | ≈ $511 |
| Category leader | 150 / 100 / 120 | ≈ $1,499 |

A perennial how-to with no direct competitor and steady search demand ("garage
organization") most plausibly lands between the first two rows — **$2–6k/year** per
language, indefinitely, for work already done.

## 6. What is deliberately NOT done

- **No color interior** (3x printing cost for marginal benefit in this genre).
- **No hardcover at launch** (adds SKU complexity; revisit if the paperback sells).
- **No $9.99+ eBook** (falls out of the 70 % band — royalty *drops*).
- **No wide distribution yet** (Select exclusivity is worth more for the first 90 days).
