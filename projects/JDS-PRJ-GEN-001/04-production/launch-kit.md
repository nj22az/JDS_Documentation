# Launch Kit — reviews, beta readers & a simple launch plan

Reviews and a strong cover are the two levers that decide whether this book sells. The cover is
briefed in `cover-brief.md`; this file gets you the first reviews and a clean launch. Everything
here is ready to send — fill the [brackets].

---

## 1. Beta readers (aim for 12–15, to get ~10 reviews)

Who to ask: people with a real garage — friends, neighbours, a local DIY/woodworking/car group,
a Facebook home-organising group, colleagues. You only need a dozen who'll actually read it.

**The ask (message / email):**

> Subject: Would you read my garage book before it launches?
>
> Hi [name] — I've written a short, practical book called *The Garage Reset*: a 10-step plan to
> clear out and organise a garage in a few weekends, based on the order method I used as an
> engineer. Before it goes live I'd love a few real readers.
>
> If you're up for it, I'll send the eBook (a PDF or ePub) for free. All I ask: if it's useful,
> leave an honest review on launch day — even two lines helps enormously. No pressure to be kind;
> honest is what helps.
>
> Want a copy? Just reply "yes" and I'll send it over.
>
> Thanks — Nils

**Then, on launch day:**

> Subject: *The Garage Reset* is live — your review would mean a lot
>
> Hi [name] — it's live: [link]. If the book helped you, a quick honest review here [review link]
> today makes a real difference to whether Amazon shows it to others. Two lines is plenty. Thank
> you for reading it early — Nils

A few tips:
- **Don't offer payment or a gift for a review** — it breaks Amazon's rules and risks the listing.
  A free advance copy is fine; the review must be unconditional and honest.
- Space the asks; 10 reviews in week one beats 50 on day one.
- Keep a simple list (name · sent? · launch-day nudge? · reviewed?) — a tab in `change-log.xlsx`
  works.

## 2. What to collect while they read

- **One-line endorsements** for the cover/【listing】 ("Finally got my car back in — Dave, Leeds").
- **The honest snags** — where did they stall, what was unclear? Last cheap chance to fix the book.
- **A photo of their result**, if willing — gold for ads and social proof.

## 3. Launch checklist (in order)

1. [ ] Final cover (see `cover-brief.md`) — eBook first.
2. [ ] 12–15 beta readers reading; endorsements collected.
3. [ ] Host the printables and set the real QR URL (`scripts/build-qr.py`) + email capture.
4. [ ] KDP listing from `amazon-listing-en.md` (title, subtitle, description, 7 keywords, 3 categories).
5. [ ] Enrol in **KDP Select** (KU + launch promos) — see `JDS-RPT-GEN-002`.
6. [ ] Upload `The-Garage-Reset.epub` + cover; preview in KDP's previewer.
7. [ ] Order a **printed proof** of the paperback before pressing publish.
8. [ ] Launch eBook at **99p/99¢ for 3–5 days**, then raise to the normal price.
9. [ ] Send the launch-day review email the morning it goes live.
10. [ ] Ads only *after* you have ~10 reviews and a converting cover.

## 4. Good launch timing (gift-driven)

New Year ("new year, sort the garage"), early spring (spring-clean), and **Father's Day** are the
natural peaks for this title. Aim a launch or a price promo at one of them.

## 5. The one thing to remember

Lead every piece of marketing with the **simple promise** — *get the car back in, in a few
weekends* — not the records, rounds, or journals. Those are the pleasant surprise inside, not the
hook. (See the readiness assessment: don't let the depth scare the casual buyer.)
