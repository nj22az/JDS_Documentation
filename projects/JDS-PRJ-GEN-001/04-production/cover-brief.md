# Cover Brief & KDP Specs

> The cover is the single biggest driver of sales in this category — treat it as the #1
> investment. `cover-en.jpg` (built by `scripts/build-cover.py`) is a **programmatic placeholder**,
> good enough to preview the layout but **not the cover to launch with.** This document briefs a
> real designer and sets the specs.

## Design brief (hand this to the designer)

- **Book:** *The Garage Reset — A 10-Step Programme to Declutter, Organise, and Keep Order in Your
  Garage and Workshop.* Author: **Nils Johansson.**
- **Positioning:** the warm, do-it-in-weekends garage book written by a real marine/field-service
  engineer — the order method (5S) professionals use, brought home. Offline; no app.
- **Target reader:** the overwhelmed homeowner whose car won't fit and who can't find a tool; plus
  the spouse buying it as a gift. Not a technical audience — keep it **inviting, not industrial.**
- **The feeling to sell:** *relief and control* — "I can actually do this, in a weekend." Aspirational
  but attainable; warm, not clinical.
- **Must-haves:** title huge and legible at **160 px thumbnail**; clear genre signal (home/DIY
  organising); author name; a clean focal image.
- **Must-avoid:** busy clip-art, industrial/cold feel, more than ~3 colours, tiny subtitles, anything
  that screams "manual" or "textbook".
- **Palette (optional starting point):** navy #13314F, amber #E8A33D, cream #F4F1EB — but let the
  designer lead; a bright, hopeful palette may convert better than dark navy.

## Three concept directions (pick one to commission)

1. **Photographic "after" (recommended).** One beautiful, well-lit shot of an *ordered* garage or a
   tidy pegboard wall, with a bold title band. Aspirational; tends to convert best in home/DIY. Buy
   a licensed stock photo or shoot one.
2. **Before → after split.** The cover itself tells the story: a cluttered half and an ordered half,
   title across the middle. Instantly communicates the promise; very "thumb-stopping".
3. **Bold typographic + one icon.** Big title, one clean motif (a single tool, or a garage-door
   outline), strong colour. Cheapest to produce; an elevated version of the current placeholder.

## Where to get it made (and rough budget)

- **Premade cover** (customised with your title): ~£30–£90. Fastest, lowest risk.
- **Freelance designer** — Reedsy, 100Covers, ebooklaunch, Fiverr Pro: ~£150–£400. Best value/quality.
- **99designs contest:** ~£300+. Many options, slower.
- **AI-generated imagery:** only if the tool's licence permits **commercial** use and you can verify
  it — KDP makes you liable for image rights. Safer to use a designer or licensed stock.

> Whichever route: give them this brief + the title/subtitle/author text, ask for the eBook cover
> first, then the wraparound once the page count is final. Always order a **printed proof** before
> publishing the paperback.
