## 1. Concept

A warm, practical handbook that does for the **garage, workshop, and storage room** what the
bestsellers did for the home — but written by a real engineer who applies **5S workshop
discipline** to the job.

- **English title (master):** *The Garage Reset* — A 10-Step Programme to Declutter, Organise,
  and Keep Order in Your Garage and Workshop
- **Swedish title:** *Städa i Garaget* — rensa, organisera och håll ordning i verkstad och förråd
- **Method words:** EN **Sort · Set in Order · Shine · Standardise · Sustain** (real 5S);
  SV Sortera · Systematisera · Städa · Standardisera · Säkra
- **Format:** project-by-project, do-it-this-weekend
- **Voice:** plain, encouraging, hands-on; method over product brands

## 2. Target Reader

| Reader | Need |
|--------|------|
| Villa & radhus owner | A garage that has become a dumping ground |
| Fritidshus owner | A förråd / uthus full of seasonal and inherited gear |
| Hobby mechanic / woodworker | A workshop that fights back instead of helping |
| Recently inherited / downsizing | A lifetime of tools and parts to sort (döstädning, but for the shed) |

## 3. The Promise

By the end, the reader has cleared the space once, built a simple **system** that keeps
itself, and knows the short routine that stops it sliding back. Not a one-time blitz — a
space that stays usable.

## 3a. The Reader's Roadmap — the 10-Step Programme

The book is sold and used as a **10-step programme**, laid out up front in
`frontmatter/03-programmet.md` and as a tear-out checklist in `backmatter/00-checklista.md`.
This is the "how it's used" spine: the reader follows ten numbered, concrete steps (one or two
per weekend, ~5 weekends total), each mapped to the chapter that details it. The 5S method
(below) is the engine *behind* the ten steps:

| # | Step | 5S phase | Chapter |
|---|------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Sätt målet (goal + before-photo) | (prep) | 1–2 |
| 2 | Töm och se | Sortera | 3 |
| 3 | Behålla, lämna, flytta | Sortera | 4 |
| 4 | Ta hand om det farliga | Sortera | 5 |
| 5 | Rita zonkartan | Systematisera | 6 |
| 6 | Upp från golvet | Systematisera | 7 |
| 7 | Märk och gör sökbart | Systematisera | 8 |
| 8 | Städa rent | Städa | 1–2 |
| 9 | Sätt reglerna | Standardisera | 9 |
| 10 | Håll det vid liv | Säkra | 10–11 |

Chapters 12–14 are special cases outside the core ten; chapter 15 closes.

## 4. The Method (the spine of the book)

The book teaches one method, taught in plain Swedish, that the reader applies room by
room. It is 5S, translated out of the factory and into the home garage:

| Step (Swedish) | Plain meaning | What the reader does |
|----------------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Sortera** | Keep / bin / move | Decide what stays, leaves, or belongs elsewhere |
| **Systematisera** | A place for everything | Zones, shelves, tool boards, labels |
| **Städa** | Clean as a baseline | A clean floor and bench is the new normal |
| **Standardisera** | Make it the default | Simple rules so it stays this way |
| **Säkra** | Keep it alive | The weekly/seasonal routine that holds the line |

> The five Swedish "S" words map cleanly onto the original method — a natural, honest hook
> that reinforces the author's engineering credibility without jargon.

## 5. Structure & Chapter Outline

### Del 1 — Tanken (The idea)

1. **"Varför garaget alltid blir värst"** (Why the garage always ends up worst) —
   how the space becomes the house's overflow valve, and why that is fixable.
2. **"Metoden på en sida"** (The method on one page) — the five steps, plainly, with a
   promise that each is a finishable weekend project.

### Del 2 — Rensa (Clear out)

3. **"Töm och se"** (Empty and look) — the one-pile reckoning; facing what's actually there.
4. **"Behålla, lämna, flytta"** (Keep, let go, relocate) — simple decision rules for tools,
   parts, paint, "might-need-it" hardware, and inherited gear.
5. **"Det som är farligt eller giftigt"** (The dangerous and the toxic) — fuels, oils,
   batteries, chemicals, gas: how to store, and how to get rid of safely (återvinning).

### Del 3 — Systemet (Build the system)

6. **"Zoner som funkar"** (Zones that work) — bilen, verktyg, trädgård, säsong, sopor;
   put each where the body actually uses it.
7. **"Upp från golvet"** (Up off the floor) — walls, tool boards, shelving, overhead;
   floor space is for working, not storing.
8. **"Hitta på tre sekunder"** (Find it in three seconds) — labelling, shadow boards,
   clear bins; the test that the system passes or fails.

### Del 4 — Håll ordning (Keep order)

9. **"Reglerna som håller"** (The rules that hold) — the few default habits that keep the
   space from refilling.
10. **"Tio minuter i veckan"** (Ten minutes a week) — the small recurring reset.
11. **"Vår och höst"** (Spring and autumn) — the two seasonal turns (däckbyte, trädgård,
    vinterförvaring) that double as natural decluttering checkpoints.

### Del 5 — Specialfall (Special cases)

12. **"Fritidshuset och uthuset"** (The holiday home and the shed) — damp, mice, the
    inherited-gear problem, the rarely-visited space.
13. **"Förrådet i lägenheten"** (The flat storage unit) — small, shared, often a
    cage in a basement; making little space honest.
14. **"När du ärver en verkstad"** (When you inherit a workshop) — the döstädning case,
    handled with respect and a plan.

### Avslutning (Close)

15. **"Ett garage som hjälper dig"** (A garage that helps you) — what good order gives
    back: time, safety, money, and a space you actually want to use.
