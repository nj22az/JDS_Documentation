# Is Your Garage Actually Working?

Before you start, six honest questions. Don't tidy anything first — answer for the garage exactly
as it is right now, this minute.

1. Can you park the car inside **today**, without moving anything first?
2. Can you find any tool you own in **under ten seconds**?
3. Is there a clear, walkable path from the door to the back wall?
4. Do you know, roughly, what's in the boxes on the top shelf?
5. Have you ever **bought something you already owned** because you couldn't find it?
6. Have you "organised the garage" before — and watched it slide back?

Count a mark against you for every **"no"** on 1–4, and every **"yes"** on 5–6.

- **0–1** — You're in good shape. Skip to the Toolkit at the back and keep it that way.
- **2–4** — A normal, fixable garage. The ten-step programme is built exactly for you.
- **5–6** — The most common score there is. Don't feel bad: it isn't a character flaw, it just
  means the room never had a system. You'll have the car back in within a few weekends.

And the honest mirror: **if you said "yes" to the last question — you've organised it before and it
crept back — you don't have a system yet. You have a recurring cleanup.** That's the single thing
this book changes. A clean garage is an afternoon; a garage that *stays* clean is a system, and a
system is what you're about to build.

One more reassurance before we begin: **your garage doesn't have to be a workshop.** Whether it's a
wall of tools or just bikes, bins, and boxes — whether you're handy or have never owned a drill —
the method is the same, and so is the result.
